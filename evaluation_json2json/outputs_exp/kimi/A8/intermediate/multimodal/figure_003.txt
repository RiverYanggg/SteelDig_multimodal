The x-axis spans approximately 500–1500 °C and the y-axis shows phase fraction from 0 to 1.0. Four distinct curves/regions are visible: ferrite (α, BCC), austenite (γ, FCC), κ-carbide (κ), and liquid (L). The α fraction decreases with falling temperature until roughly 1000 °C, then increases again; the γ phase dominates the intermediate temperature range, forming a closed loop that peaks near 0.65–0.70. The caption and citation text state that κ-carbide appears below about 823 °C via the eutectoid reaction γ → α + κ. A small amount of δ-ferrite is predicted to persist without fully transforming to austenite. The liquid phase emerges only above ~1350 °C. The plot provides the thermodynamic baseline for interpreting microstructural evolution during annealing (700–1200 °C), although the authors note that the actual measured dissolution temperature of κ-carbide (850–900 °C) is higher than the calculated equilibrium value, attributing the discrepancy to non-equilibrium conditions and the difference between atomic fractions (calculation) and volume fractions (XRD measurement).", "confidence": "high"}
