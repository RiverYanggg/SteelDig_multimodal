Both subfigures share a 0.2 μm scale bar. Subfigure (a) shows the microstructure of the quenched sample (from 1100 °C), revealing glide bands oriented in various directions with primarily straight dislocations between them. Subfigure (b) shows the furnace-cooled sample (from 1100 °C), where deformation produced dislocation tangles between the glide bands. The pronounced contrast between the straight dislocations in the quenched condition and the tangled dislocations in the furnace-cooled condition demonstrates a strong effect of thermal history on the deformation substructure.
