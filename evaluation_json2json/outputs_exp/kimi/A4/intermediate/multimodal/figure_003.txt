{"image_type": "microscopy_sem", "description": "This figure presents SEM backscattered-electron (BSE) images revealing the distribution of second-phase particles in a Fe-31Mn-9Al-1C-0.45Nb-0.45Mo-0.45W austenitic low-density steel subjected to different hot-rolling reductions. Panels (a–c) display micron-sized particles at lower magnification (scale bar 20 μm), while panels (d–f) show submicron-sized particles at higher magnification (scale bar 300 nm). The sample correspondence is (a, d) HR45 (45% reduction), (b, e) HR68 (68% reduction), and (c, f) HR82 (82% reduction). In (a–c), bright polygonal micron-sized particles are aligned along the rolling direction, primarily at austenite grain boundaries and in grain interiors. In (d–f), submicron-sized spherical particles are dispersed within the matrix. The associated text identifies these as (Nb,Mo,W)C complex carbide particles. Comparison across the three conditions indicates that increasing hot-rolling reduction refines the austenite grain size while retaining these carbide particles, which serve as preferential nucleation sites for microcracks yet contribute to precipitation strengthening and particle-stimulated nucleation of dynamic recrystallization.", "confidence": "high"}
