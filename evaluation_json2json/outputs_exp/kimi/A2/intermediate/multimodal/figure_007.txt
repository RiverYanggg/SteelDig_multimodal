{"image_type": "microscopy_tem", "description": "This transmission electron micrograph shows the dislocation substructure in a Fe–20% Al alloy after room-temperature compression to 2–3% plastic strain. The image reveals straight screw dislocations, some loosely paired, along with edge dipole segments and small dislocation loops distributed across the thin foil. The micrograph was recorded near the [0 0 1] zone axis with an imaging g vector of 1 1 0, as indicated by the arrow, and the scale bar represents 0.2 μm. According to the caption and text, these dislocations have a Burgers vector of (1/2)<1 1 1> and represent single dislocations characteristic of disordered or short-range ordered Fe–Al alloys containing 15–20% Al. The narrow edge dipoles and small loops are reported to be produced by trailing from gliding screw dislocations. This figure supports the paper's argument that low-Al-content Fe–Al alloys deform by individual dislocation motion, contrasting with the superdislocation-mediated deformation observed in higher-Al ordered alloys, thereby contributing to the understanding of composition-dependent work hardening mechanisms.", "confidence": "high"}
