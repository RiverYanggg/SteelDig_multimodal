{"image_type": "property_mechanical", "description": "Figure 2 plots the variation of work hardening rate (WHR), measured between 1 and 1.5% plastic strain, as a function of aluminum content for polycrystalline as-cast or annealed Fe-Al alloys. The chart contains two data series: solid circles denoting binary Fe-Al alloys and dashed squares denoting Fe-Al-B alloys containing about 0.1% B. The WHR remains low (roughly 1–2 GPa) for disordered alloys with less than ~20% Al, increases steeply through the 20–28% Al range, reaches a sharp maximum of approximately 16 GPa at 28–29% Al, and then declines to moderate values above ~35% Al. The Fe-Al-B data points indicate only a slight increase relative to the B-free samples, with little consistent change in the work hardening rate. This composition dependence supports the conclusion that the ordered state exhibits significantly higher work hardening than the disordered state, with the pronounced peak near 28–29% Al reflecting superdislocation dipole hardening mechanisms and a high density of straight screw-oriented superdislocations in the ordered DO3/B2 structures.", "confidence": "high"}
