Panels (a) and (b) display reconstructed experimental EBSD grain-orientation maps (upper rows) and corresponding GOS maps (lower rows) at true strains of 0.5 and 0.9, respectively. Under constant 1050 °C, the average grain size (AGS) is 80.01 ± 4.30 μm at 0.5 strain with a DRX fraction of 98.6%, increasing slightly to 81.30 ± 4.39 μm at 0.9 strain. In contrast, the increasing-temperature path (950 to 1050 °C in the strain range 0.4–0.5) yields a finer microstructure at 0.5 strain (62.63 ± 4.11 μm, DRX 97.3%) that coarsens to 79.69 ± 4.78 μm by 0.9 strain. Panels (c) and (d) show 3D cellular-automaton simulations for the constant and varying-temperature conditions, respectively. The constant-temperature simulation predicts AGS values of 79.79 μm (0.5 strain, 98.2% DRX) and 79.89 μm (0.9 strain, 98.6% DRX), while the increasing-temperature simulation gives 65.61 μm (0.5 strain, 99.5% DRX) and 75.90 μm (0.9 strain, 99.8% DRX), with green and red grains distinguishing the first (950 °C) and second (1050 °C) DRX stages.
