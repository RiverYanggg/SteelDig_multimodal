{"image_type": "microscopy_sem", "description": "This scanning electron micrograph displays the surface morphology of oxide protrusions formed on Fe-15Mn-8Al alloy following isothermal oxidation at 800°C for 24 hr in pure oxygen at 200 torr. The micrograph reveals a dense and relatively uniform population of small nodular protrusions distributed across the oxidized surface, which the accompanying text reports are typically less than one micron in diameter. Unlike the larger bulky oxides characteristic of region II alloys, the caption and discussion state that their spatial distribution is not structurally related to the underlying alloy microstructure. The text further notes that few such protrusions are observed on low-manganese alloys, whereas they become abundant at high manganese concentrations, as clearly illustrated by this Fe-15Mn-8Al specimen. These small manganese-rich oxide features represent a distinct morphological component associated with protective alumina-forming regions, supporting the core conclusion that elevated manganese contents promote the nucleation and growth of fine surface oxide outgrowths on otherwise protective scales.", "confidence": "high"}
