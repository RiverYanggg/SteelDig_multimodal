Subfigure (a) shows a hollow, convoluted oxide mound formed after 24 hr, exhibiting a highly porous and cellular internal structure with numerous voids and cavities. Subfigure (b) shows the surface after 100 hr oxidation, revealing oxide threads adjacent to spalled alumina regions, together with numerous small manganese-rich oxide nodules. According to the caption and text, this alloy contains 4% aluminum—below the threshold required to form continuous protective alumina scales in this system—and therefore develops non-protective hollow oxide mounds. After 100 hr, all alloys exhibited scale degradation; the presence of oxide threads and spalled alumina indicates breakdown of the initially formed scale.
