{"image_type": "diffraction_xrd", "description": "Figure 2 presents X-ray diffraction patterns comparing phase evolution in two Fe-Mn-Al-C-(Ni) low-density steels: (a) Ni-free 28Mn0Ni and (b) Ni-containing 15Mn5Ni, following hot rolling plus cold rolling (HR+CR) and subsequent annealing at 973 K or 1173 K. In the as-cold-rolled state, both alloys exhibit only austenite γ (FCC) peaks. After annealing at 973 K, additional diffraction peaks emerge in both alloys, corresponding to κ-carbide (red circles) and B2 intermetallic (blue triangles), confirming concurrent precipitation. The B2 peaks are markedly more intense in 15Mn5Ni than in 28Mn0Ni, indicating that Ni addition promotes B2 formation, especially at lower Mn levels. After higher-temperature annealing at 1173 K, the 28Mn0Ni alloy reverts to a single γ-phase pattern, indicating dissolution of both κ-carbide and B2 precipitates. In contrast, 15Mn5Ni retains pronounced B2 peaks at 1173 K while κ-carbide peaks disappear, demonstrating higher thermal stability of B2 in the Ni-bearing, lower-Mn composition. These results establish that Mn/Ni ratio strongly dictates precipitation sequences and dissolution temperatures during post-deformation heat treatment.", "confidence": "high"}
