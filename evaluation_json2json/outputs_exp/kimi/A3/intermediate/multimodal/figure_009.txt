Subfigure (a) plots hardness versus cold rolling reduction (0–50% reduction in thickness). All alloys exhibit increasing hardness with deformation; 15Mn5Ni starts highest (~380 VHN at 0%) but work-hardens such that all three converge to ~430–460 VHN at 50% reduction. Subfigure (b) plots hardness versus post-cold-rolling heat treatment temperature (873–1273 K, 1 h WQ). Hardness generally decreases with increasing annealing temperature. The Ni-free 28Mn0Ni shows the steepest drop (from ~430 VHN at 873 K to ~190 VHN at 1273 K), while 15Mn5Ni maintains the highest hardness across the entire window (~500 VHN at 873 K to ~300 VHN at 1273 K), attributed to higher volume fractions of B2 intermetallics and κ-carbides that retard softening. 28Mn5Ni shows intermediate behavior.
