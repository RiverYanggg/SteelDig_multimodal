  "description": "Figure 12 presents four TEM micrographs showing slip bands in Fe–28Mn–9Al–1.2C-(0,6)Cr austenitic low-density steel strips after tensile fracture. Subfigure (a) displays the as-cast 0Cr strip with an average slip-band spacing of ~90 nm, whereas (b) shows the annealed 0Cr strip with a widened spacing of ~190 nm. Subfigure (c) depicts the as-cast 6Cr strip with a spacing of ~80 nm, and (d) shows the annealed 6Cr strip with a refined spacing of ~60 nm. The divergent evolution upon annealing—spacing increases in 0Cr but decreases in 6Cr—correlates with opposite changes in elongation. The accompanying text reports that average slip-band widths are 13.4 nm and 12.5 nm for as-cast 0Cr and 6Cr, and 20.5 nm and 7.9 nm after annealing, respectively. The authors attribute the coarser spacing in annealed 0Cr to precipitation of ~14 nm κ-carbides that obstruct dislocations, while finer, less abundant κ-carbides in 6Cr facilitate multi-directional shearing and denser slip bands. These micrographs support that slip-band spacing is governed by κ-carbide precipitation state and initial dislocation density, controlling strain hardening and ductility.",
