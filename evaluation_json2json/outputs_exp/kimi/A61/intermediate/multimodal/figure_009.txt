Subfigures (a) and (e) show the ST sample, (b) and (f) show 550-6, (c) and (g) show 600-6, and (d) and (h) show 650-6. The ST, 550-6, and 600-6 samples exhibit predominant dimpled fracture surfaces across both stress amplitudes, indicating ductile microvoid coalescence failure mechanisms. In contrast, the 650-6 sample displays mixed-mode fracture characteristics featuring both dimples and cleavage-faceted regions at both 180 MPa and 315 MPa, as visibly evident in (d) and (h). The caption and citation text report that this transition from ductile to mixed brittle-ductile fracture in 650-6 is attributed to extensive intergranular κ-carbide precipitation, which reduces ductility and promotes cleavage-like faceting, whereas the intragranular precipitation states in 550-6 and 600-6 preserve toughness. These results support the conclusion that the precipitation state of κ-carbides critically governs fatigue damage tolerance and final fracture behavior.", "confidence": "high"}
