{"image_type": "chart_schematic", "description": "Figure 12 presents a schematic diagram comparing the slip reversibility mechanisms in Fe-30.5Mn-8Al-1C austenitic low-density steel under solution-treated (ST) and aged conditions. Panel (a) illustrates the ST condition where short-range ordering (SRO) within the austenitic matrix causes glide plane softening; however, the relatively weak and spatially heterogeneous SRO structure is prematurely destroyed by repeated dislocation shearing, leading to localized absence of order, non-uniform softening across potential slip planes, strain concentration, and consequently low slip reversibility. Panel (b) depicts the aged condition where coherent ordered L1₂-structured intragranular κ-carbides are uniformly dispersed. When moving dislocations shear these precipitates, anti-phase boundaries (APBs) are created; subsequent dislocations traverse the same softened planes with reduced resistance because additional APBs are no longer formed. This path-dependent process channels dislocation activity to a limited set of previously softened glide planes, strengthens slip planarity, suppresses cross-slip, and significantly improves the reversibility of dislocation motion under cyclic loading, thereby reducing accumulated damage per cycle. This mechanism rationalizes the superior fatigue performance of the aged samples relative to the ST samples.", "confidence": "high"}
