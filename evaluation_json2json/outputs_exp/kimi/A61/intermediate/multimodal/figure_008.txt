Each subfigure includes a higher-magnification inset with a 10 μm scale bar revealing detailed fatigue striation morphology, while the main images use a 100 μm scale bar. All specimens exhibit well-defined fatigue striations characteristic of transgranular crack advancement. The 550-6 and 600-6 samples display more pronounced and closely spaced striations relative to ST and 650-6, with caption-reported narrowest spacing in 600-6 at 180 MPa and 550-6 at 315 MPa. The 650-6 condition shows comparatively wider striation spacing, consistent with coarse intergranular κ-carbide precipitation accelerating crack growth. These morphological differences support that optimal intragranular κ-carbide precipitation homogenizes plastic deformation and impedes fatigue crack propagation, whereas over-aging and intergranular precipitation degrade fatigue crack resistance.", "confidence": "high"}
