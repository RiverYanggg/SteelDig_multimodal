{"image_type": "property_mechanical", "description": "Figure 3 presents a four-panel overview of mechanical properties from Rapid Alloy Prototyping (RAP) experiments on Fe-30Mn-1.2C-xAl austenitic steels (x = 0, 2, 4, 6, 8 wt.% Al) as a function of aluminum content and aging treatment. Subplots (a), (b), (c), and (d) plot yield stress (YS, MPa), ultimate tensile stress (UTS, MPa), total elongation (TE, %), and hardness (HBW), respectively. Data series include the as-homogenised condition and various aging treatments: 450°C/1h, 500°C/1h, 550°C/1h, 600°C/1h, 500°C/24h, 550°C/24h, and 600°C/24h. For Al contents up to 6 wt.%, all properties remain relatively stable across treatments. At 8 wt.% Al, a pronounced divergence occurs: prolonged aging at 550–600°C (particularly 550°C/24h and 600°C/24h) causes sharp increases in YS (~800–950 MPa), UTS (~850–950 MPa), and hardness (~320–360 HBW), but precipitously drops TE to ~10%. Conversely, the as-homogenised and short-term/low-temperature aged 8Al alloys retain high ductility (~60–80% TE) with modest strength. This trade-off confirms that kappa-carbide precipitation during intermediate-temperature aging of high-Al compositions provides substantial age-hardening at the cost of tensile ductility.", "confidence": "high"}
