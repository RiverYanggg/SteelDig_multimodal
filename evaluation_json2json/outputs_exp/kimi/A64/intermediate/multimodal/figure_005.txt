Subfigure (a) shows an APT three-dimensional reconstruction where κ-carbides (L'1₂ structure) are rendered as purple iso-surfaces at 9 at.% C, embedded in a point cloud of C, Mn, and Al atoms with crystallographic directions [001] and [100] indicated. Below the reconstruction, a one-dimensional compositional depth profile extracted along a cylindrical region traversing multiple precipitates displays concentration oscillations of Fe, Mn, Al, and C (at.% versus distance in nm), revealing C and Al enrichment and Fe depletion within the κ-carbide zones. Subfigure (b) is a TEM micrograph (scale bar 20 nm) showing the regularly arranged nanosized cuboidal or rod-like κ-carbides within the austenitic matrix. Together, these data confirm the nanoscale dimensions, periodic distribution, and approximate (Fe,Mn)₃AlC stoichiometry of the κ-carbides. As stated in the caption and text, these precipitates are thermally very stable at 600°C, persisting after 100 h with only modest coarsening, and the associated deformation mechanism involves Orowan bypassing of longitudinal carbide rods.", "confidence": "high"}
