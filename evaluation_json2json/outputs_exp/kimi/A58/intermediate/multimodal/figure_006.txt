At 3 % strain, austenite (a) shows planar dislocation slip on {111} planes with cross-slip, while ferrite (b,c) exhibits slip bands composed of edge, mixed, and screw dislocations on {112} planes. At 10 %, cross-slip intensifies in austenite (d) and slip bands evolve in ferrite (e). At 20 %, austenite forms dislocation cell blocks (f) and dislocation cells (g), whereas ferrite slip bands refine (h). At 28 %, austenite displays further refined cell blocks (i), cells (j), and deformation twins (k), while ferrite (l) shows refined slip bands with cross-slip. SAED insets confirm the γ [110] and [100] zone axes and α [110] and [011] axes, with DO3 superlattice reflections visible in ferrite patterns.
