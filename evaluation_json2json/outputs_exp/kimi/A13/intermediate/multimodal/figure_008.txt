Subfigure (a) displays XRD profiles of specimens treated at 700 °C, 800 °C, and 900 °C before and after tensile deformation (post-deformation labeled with -F). Prior to deformation, diffraction peaks correspond to austenite (γ) including (111), (200), (220), and (311), ferrite/martensite (α) including (110), (200), and (211), and M5C2 carbide. Initial austenite fractions increase from 12.2% (H70) to 20.7% (H80) and 33.8% (H90), decreasing after deformation to 4.5%, 5.8%, and 3.4%, respectively. Corresponding martensitic transformation ratios are 63.1%, 71.9%, and 89.9%.
