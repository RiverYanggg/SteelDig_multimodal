Panel (a) plots temperature (500–900 °C) versus Al content (5–11 wt.%) for Fe-30Mn-xAl-1C alloys, and panel (b) plots temperature versus Mn content (23–30 wt.%) for Fe-xMn-7Al-1C alloys. Both panels display three phase fields: single-phase austenite (γ), γ plus intergranular κ-phase (γ + κ*), and γ plus intergranular and intragranular κ-phase (γ + κ + κ*), where caption identifies κ as intragranular and κ* as intergranular κ-carbide. In panel (a), increasing Al from approximately 5.5 to 10 wt.% markedly raises the upper solvus boundary from about 735 °C to over 830 °C and shifts the lower γ + κ* / γ + κ + κ* boundary upward from roughly 545 °C to 740 °C. Conversely, panel (b) shows that raising Mn from about 23 to 30 wt.% slightly depresses the solvus, with the upper boundary decreasing from ~800 °C to ~765 °C and the lower boundary from ~690 °C to ~665 °C. These opposing trends indicate that Al strongly stabilizes κ-carbide and elevates its dissolution temperature, whereas Mn mildly lowers it, supporting alloy design for precipitation hardening in the 500–750 °C aging window.", "confidence": "high"}
