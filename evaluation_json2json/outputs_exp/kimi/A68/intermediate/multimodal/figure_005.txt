Four distinct curves are plotted: ferrite (red), austenite (blue), carbides (magenta), and liquid (black). Ferrite constitutes approximately 95 wt.% at low temperatures, undergoes a step decrease to around 88 wt.% near 700°C as austenite nucleates, then increases again toward 100 wt.% before the alloy melts. Austenite emerges near 700°C, peaks at roughly 10 wt.%, and continuously decreases until fully dissolving near 1250°C. Carbides precipitate below approximately 650°C at levels near 2 wt.% before complete dissolution. Liquid appears above about 1450°C and rises sharply. The figure demonstrates the inverse intercritical-phase-field behavior characteristic of lean Fe-Mn-Al-C duplex steels: austenite content decreases while ferrite content increases with rising temperature, contrary to standard low-carbon steels.
