Six replicate curves are shown: LN1 (green dashed), LN2 (grey dashed), and LN3 (yellow dotted) for the LN steel, and HN1 (black solid), HN2 (red solid), and HN3 (blue solid) for the HN steel. All specimens exhibit continuous yielding followed by pronounced strain hardening and necking prior to fracture. Visually, the HN steel curves extend to markedly higher strains before fracture, whereas the LN curves terminate at lower elongations, indicating earlier failure during local necking. The accompanying caption and text report specific mechanical values: the HN steel achieves an ultimate tensile strength of 841.5 MPa and an elongation of 74.5%, compared with 832.5 MPa and 62.5% for the LN steel, while the yield strength decreases from 685 MPa to 647 MPa due to reduced ferrite content with nitrogen addition. The consistently larger area under the HN curves indicates greater energy absorption during fracture.
