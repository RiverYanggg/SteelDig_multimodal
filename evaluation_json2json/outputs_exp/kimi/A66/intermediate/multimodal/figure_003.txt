Panel (a) shows the 0.59% Si steel and panel (b) shows the 1.56% Si steel, both aged at 490°C for 63 h as stated in the caption. The bright nanoscale features dispersed throughout the dark austenitic matrix are identified by the caption as j-carbides (κ-carbides). Visual inspection and the caption both indicate that increasing silicon from 0.59% to 1.56% results in a noticeably higher number density and larger size of these carbide precipitates. The SAED insets provide corresponding crystallographic information, although individual diffraction spot indices remain unreadable at the given image resolution. This direct microstructural evidence supports the core finding that silicon accelerates κ-carbide formation and increases its volume fraction, which the citing text reports raises the carbon partitioning coefficient and the constrained austenite-κ-carbide mismatch to produce enhanced age hardening.", "confidence": "high"}
