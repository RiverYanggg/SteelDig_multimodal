  "description": "Figure 12 consists of two scanning electron microscopy (SEM) micrographs displaying the fracture surfaces of Fe-30Mn-9Al-0.9C lightweight austenitic steels with different silicon contents after aging and cryogenic impact testing. Subfigure (a) shows the 1.07% Si specimen and (b) shows the 1.56% Si specimen, both aged for 12 h at 530-C and broken at -40-C as stated in the caption. Both images reveal predominantly transgranular cleavage-like fracture morphology at the 20 μm scale, with distinct slip traces visible on certain favorably oriented cleavage facets. The micrographs demonstrate that increasing silicon content does not change the primary brittle fracture mode at low temperature but correlates with reduced notch toughness, consistent with reported Charpy energy decreases from 92 J to 68 J at room temperature and an average 15 J reduction at -40-C. The observed slip traces support the proposed slipping-off mechanism via glide plane decohesion along {111} planes associated with planar slip and k-carbide precipitation during aging. Together, these images provide direct morphological evidence that silicon additions promote brittle transgranular cleavage fracture in aged high-Mn steels under cryogenic conditions.",
