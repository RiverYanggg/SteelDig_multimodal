The GND density monotonically decreases from HR toward higher annealing temperatures in both phases, with the BCC phase consistently showing higher values (from 10.81 to 4.63 × 10^14 m^-2) than the FCC phase (from 8.37 to 2.66 × 10^14 m^-2), reflecting progressive recovery and stress relief during tempering. The caption and associated text note that AG boundary density rises with annealing temperature but drops at 800 °C due to austenite grain coarsening, whereas block boundary density continuously decreases. The loss of block boundaries and the replacement by AG boundaries as reverted austenite grows are linked to the decline in yield strength of the annealed specimens.", "confidence": "high"}
