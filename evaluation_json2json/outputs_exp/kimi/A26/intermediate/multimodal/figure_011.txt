{"image_type": "microscopy_sem", "description": "Figure 10 presents scanning electron microscopy (SEM) images of fracture vertical-section microstructures from fracture toughness specimens of low alloy ultrahigh strength steels. Panels (a) and (b) are low-magnification images with a 50 μm scale bar for the 0Al and 1.3Al steel specimens, respectively, where cyan arrows trace the main crack paths. The 0Al steel in (a) exhibits a relatively straight main crack with minimal deflection and sparse micro-voids. In contrast, the 1.3Al steel in (b) shows frequent crack deflections and a higher density of micro-voids along the crack path. Panels (c) and (d) are magnified images with a 10 μm scale bar corresponding to the blue boxed regions in (a) and (b), respectively. Panel (c) reveals crack tip passivation in the 0Al steel, while panel (d) for the 1.3Al steel displays secondary cracks and micro-voids (approximately 5–10 μm) located beneath the main crack, with fine cracks interconnecting these voids indicating coalescence. The comparison demonstrates that the 1.3Al steel undergoes extensive crack deflection, secondary cracking, and void coalescence, which increase crack propagation distance and promote energy dissipation. These microstructural observations support the core conclusion that high-density NiAl clusters enhance toughness by inducing crack tortuosity and blunting mechanisms, consistent with the reported higher crack propagation energy and fracture toughness in the 1.3Al steel relative to the 0Al counterpart.", "confidence": "high"}
