The citation explains that high-density dislocations in twin martensite offer nucleation sites for the ferritic transformation, so the resulting α-ferrite inherits the morphology of the prior martensite. With prolonged annealing, the film austenite located between the needle-like α-ferrite and/or prior martensite becomes thickened, implying an increase in austenite fraction. These images support the conclusion that annealing duration governs the decomposition of prior martensite into needle-like α-ferrite and the reverse transformation to austenite, which refines austenite grains and enhances austenite stability, thereby contributing to the fine-grained duplex microstructure and the good strength-ductility match in air-cooled annealed samples.", "confidence": "high"}
