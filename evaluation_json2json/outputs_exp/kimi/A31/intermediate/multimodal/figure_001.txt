Four conditions are compared: 2Al-450°C/1h (black), 2Al-650°C/72h (red), 8Al-450°C/1h (green), and 8Al-600°C/24h (blue). The short-annealed 2Al steel exhibits a yield strength of ~420 MPa, an ultimate tensile strength (UTS) of ~935 MPa, and the highest ductility (~75% elongation). Long annealing of 2Al at 650°C reduces both strength (~850 MPa UTS) and elongation (~35%), which is linked to intergranular M3C carbide precipitation. The 8Al-450°C/1h sample shows ~540 MPa yield strength with ~65% elongation. In contrast, 8Al-600°C/24h achieves the highest strength (~990 MPa yield, ~1180 MPa UTS) but lower ductility (~37%), reflecting effective precipitation strengthening by nanosized j-carbides. These curves demonstrate that Al content and annealing state critically govern the strength–ductility balance in austenitic high-Mn low-density steels.", "confidence": "high"}
