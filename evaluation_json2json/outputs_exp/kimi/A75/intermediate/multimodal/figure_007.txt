In (a), the undeformed microstructure (scale bar 50 μm) consists of large equiaxed austenite grains delineated mainly by solid black high-angle grain boundaries (HAGBs), with red low-angle grain boundaries (LAGBs) being scarce. After 30% CR (b, 20 μm), LAGBs densely populate the interiors of elongated grains, signaling intensive dislocation activity and grain subdivision. At 90% CR (c, 5 μm), the structure transforms into heavily fragmented, rolling-direction-aligned bands with an extremely high density of LAGBs and only residual HAGBs. Panel (d) plots the misorientation distribution: the undeformed steel shows a dominant peak near 60° attributed to annealing twin boundaries, while the deformed states exhibit strong low-angle peaks; citation text reports LAGB fractions rise to 89.4% at CR 30% and 94.1% at CR 90%.
