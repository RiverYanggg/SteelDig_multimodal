Panels (a), (c), and (e) are bright-field (BF) images showing the austenite matrix in the 0Cr, 3Cr, and 6Cr strips, respectively, with insets displaying selected-area diffraction patterns (SADP) along the [110] zone axis where the circled (001) superlattice reflection confirms the presence of ordered κ-carbides. Panels (b), (d), and (f) are dark-field (DF) images formed using the (001) superlattice reflection to map the spatial distribution of nano-scale κ-carbides. The DF images reveal a marked decrease in both the number density and size of κ-carbides with increasing Cr content: the 0Cr strip shows a high density of bright κ-carbide particles, the 3Cr strip shows noticeably fewer, and the 6Cr strip shows virtually no discernible κ-carbide precipitation. The caption and accompanying text report that the annealed 0Cr strip contains κ-carbides of ~7 vol.% with an average size of 8 nm, while the 3Cr strip contains ~4 vol.% and 4 nm. These observations support the conclusion that Cr addition suppresses κ-carbide precipitation by expanding the solid solubility of Al and C, which reduces the driving force for κ-carbide formation and consequently lowers the yield strength of the annealed Cr-containing steels.", "confidence": "high"}
