Subfigures (a1) and (b1) show the Fe 2p3/2 region deconvoluted into Fe3O4 (712.80 eV, 32.51% and 713.10 eV, 32.87%), Fe2O3 (710.08 eV, 40.94% and 710.12 eV, 40.74%), and FeOOH (711.31 eV, 26.55% and 711.41 eV, 26.39%) for 0Cu and 3Cu, respectively. Subfigures (a2) and (b2) display Mn 2p3/2 spectra comprising MnO (640.68 eV, 54.11% and 640.72 eV, 63.84%) and MnO2 (642.59 eV, 45.89% and 642.58 eV, 36.16%). Al 2p spectra in (a3) and (b3) reveal Al2O3 (73.76 eV, 66.48% and 73.68 eV, 60.75%) and metallic Al (71.63 eV, 33.52% and 71.72 eV, 39.25%). O 1s spectra in (a4) and (b4) consist of C=O, OH−, and O2− components. Notably, (b5) for 3Cu shows Cu 2p3/2 peaks assigned to Cu2O (932.61 eV, 66.43%) and CuO (934.71 eV, 33.57%), which are absent in 0Cu. The 0Cu passive film exhibits a higher proportion of Al2O3 and more Fe/Al oxides overall, whereas 3Cu contains Cu oxides and an increased MnO fraction. These results indicate that Cu alloying introduces unstable Cu oxides into the passive film and reduces the protective Al-rich oxide proportion, consistent with the reported intensified microgalvanic corrosion and accelerated anodic dissolution in the 3Cu steel.",
