The first subplot corresponds to Fe-30Mn-1.2C-xAl alloys, while the second corresponds to Fe-30Mn-0.4C-xAl alloys, all solutionized at 1100 °C for 2 h. Solid curves represent water-quenched samples, while dotted curves represent samples that are water-quenched then aged at 550 °C for 24 h in air. Key trends: For the 1.2C series, aging induces significant hardening only when Al content exceeds 8 wt%, which is attributed to κ'-carbide precipitation. For the 0.4C series, YS and UTS decrease while TE increases as Al rises from 0 to 2 wt% (attributed to fully austenitic microstructure formation and increased stacking fault energy), followed by increasing strength and decreasing TE with further Al addition, as δ-ferrite fraction increases linearly with Al. Aging has negligible effect on tensile properties of the 0.4C series.
