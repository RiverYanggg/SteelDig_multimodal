For each sample: (a,d,g,j) are band contrast (BC) and inverse pole figure (IPF) superimposed maps showing grain orientation and crack morphology; (b,e,h,k) are kernel average misorientation (KAM) maps quantifying local plastic strain distribution; (c,f,i,l) are Schmid factor (SF) maps for {111}<110> slip systems. All samples exhibit transgranular crack propagation along slip bands. The ST sample has a relatively straight crack path and highest KAM intensity near cracks/grain boundaries indicating severe strain localization. Aged samples show more tortuous crack paths with frequent microbranching: 600-6 has the most diffuse KAM distribution (homogeneous plastic accommodation), while 650-6 shows KAM hot spots at intergranular κ-carbide decorated grain boundaries. SF maps confirm cracks nucleate in low-SF hard-oriented grains and propagate into high-SF soft-oriented grains. This figure supports that well-dispersed intragranular κ-carbides enhance fatigue crack propagation resistance via increased crack tortuosity and reduced strain localization, while excessive intergranular κ-carbides degrade performance.",
