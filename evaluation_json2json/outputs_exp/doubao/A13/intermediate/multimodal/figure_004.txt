Subfigures (a), (b), (c), (d) correspond to true strains of 0.03, 0.10, 0.20, and 0.27 respectively, with a unified scale bar of 5 μm. In the maps, green regions represent austenite, gray regions represent ferrite, and dark regions with poor band contrast are deformation-induced martensite. Red squares mark blocky retained austenite at ferrite triple junction boundaries, while red ellipses mark lath-like retained austenite distributed between ferrite laths or inside ferrite grains. With increasing true strain, the austenite volume fraction decreases sequentially from 31.4% to 22.3%, 10.1%, and finally 4.7%: blocky austenite transforms to martensite first, leaving only lath-like austenite at the highest strain. The fraction of small angle grain boundaries also increases from 26% to over 51% with rising strain. These observations confirm that continuous deformation-induced martensitic transformation (TRIP effect) is the core mechanism for the excellent strength-ductility combination of the tested steel.",
