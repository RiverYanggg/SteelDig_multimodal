Subplot (a) shows true stress (in MPa) versus true strain curves, while subplot (b) plots work hardening rate (dσ/dε) as a function of true strain. No yield point phenomenon is observed for all tested specimens. With the increase of solution temperature, both the critical stress for uniform deformation and work hardening rate decrease obviously. The specimen treated at 900°C (H90) shows an abrupt true stress rise at true strain of ~0.03, and achieves the highest ultimate tensile strength and total elongation among all samples. All specimens exhibit three-stage work hardening characteristics: Stage 1 with rapidly decreasing work hardening rate associated with ferrite deformation, Stage 2 with slightly fluctuating work hardening rate derived from TRIP effect, Stage 3 with fast decreasing work hardening rate related to deformation localization.
