For all samples, slip band spacing decreases with increasing engineering strain initially. At an engineering strain of 0.1, the slip band spacing of HR45, HR68, HR82 is 296.40 ± 50.06 nm, 155.02 ± 38.42 nm, 68.05 ± 8.91 nm respectively, showing that higher rolling reduction (and finer initial grain size) leads to smaller initial slip band spacing. All samples reach a saturation slip band spacing of ~50 nm when engineering strain reaches ~0.2, with no further reduction at higher strain. These results confirm that finer grain size accelerates slip band refinement, and the saturation of slip band refinement at ~50 nm explains the strain hardening behavior differences among samples, supporting the dynamic slip band refinement model.",
