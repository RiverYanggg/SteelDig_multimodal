    "description": "This group of optical micrographs presents the microstructure of Fe–22Mn–0.4C–1.5Al–1.5Si (wt%) low-density steel fabricated via metal injection molding (MIM), sintered at 1360 °C for different durations. Subfigure (a) corresponds to the sample sintered for 1 h, and subfigure (b) corresponds to the sample sintered for 3 h, both with a 200 μm scale bar. Two characteristic constituents are clearly identified: equiaxed austenitic matrix grains, and dark manganese oxide particles distributed mainly along grain boundaries and partially inside grains. Comparative observation shows that extending the sintering duration from 1 h to 3 h leads to obvious growth of austenitic grains, while manganese oxide particles maintain uniform distribution. The figure verifies that MIM followed by high-temperature sintering can produce fully austenitic low-density steel with evenly dispersed manganese oxide inclusions, which is expected to improve the wear resistance of this type of structural steel.",
