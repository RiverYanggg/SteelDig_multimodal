For AC samples, the pre-annealing microstructure comprises δ-ferrite, austenite, and α' martensite. During annealing, α' martensite undergoes reverse transformation to austenite and decomposes into needle-like α-ferrite that refines austenite grains, yielding a final annealed microstructure of δ-ferrite, austenite, and α-ferrite, with equilibrium austenite fraction >65% and no post-annealing martensitic transformation. For FC samples, the pre-annealing microstructure contains only δ-ferrite and austenite; during annealing, austenite grains coarsen with only minor equiaxed α-ferrite formation, leading to lower austenite stability, so partial austenite transforms to α' martensite during final cooling, resulting in a 4-phase annealed microstructure. This difference explains the superior strength-ductility synergy of AC annealed samples enabled by stable refined austenite and TRIP effect, versus poorer ductility of FC samples from martensite-induced crack nucleation.",
