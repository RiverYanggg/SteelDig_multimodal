The first image (corresponding to 0.001 wt.% P steel) has a 10 nm scale bar, while the second image (corresponding to 0.043 wt.% P steel) has a 25 nm scale bar. Selected area electron diffraction patterns are inset in the upper right of each image to confirm the presence of perovskite-structured κ-carbides. Comparison shows κ-carbides in low-P steel are very small (only a few unit cells in size) with high number density, while κ-carbides in high-P steel have a larger average size of ~5 nm, higher number density, and preferentially coarsen along <100> crystallographic directions. This figure confirms phosphorus significantly accelerates κ-carbide precipitation and spinodal decomposition during aging, supporting the mechanism of phosphorus-induced embrittlement in this high Mn-Al steel system.",
