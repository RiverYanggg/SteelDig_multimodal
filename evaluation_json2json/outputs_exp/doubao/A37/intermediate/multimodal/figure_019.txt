    "description": "This figure is a schematic illustration of hydrogen-induced microband (MB) nucleation mechanisms in austenitic Fe30Mn6.5Al0.3C low-density steel. Subfigure (a) depicts the Geometrically Necessary Boundary (GNB)-assisted MB nucleation mechanism: hydrogen induces GNB splitting, and shear localization along GNBs (with incidental dislocation boundaries, IDBs) promotes MB nucleation. Hydrogen charging increases GNB misorientation (2~8°) compared to uncharged samples (2~3°), caused by enhanced storage of geometrically necessary dislocations under hydrogen-induced localized shear. Subfigure (b) illustrates the Deformation Band (DB)-assisted MB nucleation mechanism: hydrogen induces strain localization inside DBs, where secondary slip systems shear the primary dislocation structure (dislocation cells, DCs; dense dislocation walls, DDWs) to form MB via localized shear. These two MB nucleation mechanisms contribute to internal stress relaxation of deformation structures, alleviate hydrogen-induced strain localization damage, and improve the hydrogen embrittlement resistance of the studied steel.",
