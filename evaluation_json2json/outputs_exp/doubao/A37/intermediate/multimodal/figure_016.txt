Subfigure (a) has a scale bar of 250 nm, while (b), (c), (d) have 100 nm scale bars. Multiple dislocation configurations are labeled: A for groups of closely spaced screw dislocations, DDW for dense dislocation walls, D for dissolved DDW segments, B for DDWs, C for paired screw dislocation configurations, J for dislocation cusps with jogs, CS for cross-slipped and double-cross slipped dislocations. A high dislocation density of ~1-3×10^14 m⁻² is observed within DBs, together with active dislocation cross-slip, DDW dissolution, and dislocation interactions forming Lomer-Cottrell locks. The observations confirm intense hydrogen-promoted plastic activity inside DBs, supporting the hydrogen-enhanced microbanding (HEMB) mechanism: cross-slip and massive glide of dislocation groups shear pre-existing dislocation structures, facilitating microband nucleation and plastic relaxation, thus improving the hydrogen embrittlement resistance of the steel.",
