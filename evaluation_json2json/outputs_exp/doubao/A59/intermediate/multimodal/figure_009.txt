Subgraphs (a, b) are optical microscopy (OM) images of impact fracture deformation zones, (c) is OM of tensile fracture deformation zone, all with 10 μm scale bars. Subgraphs (d-g) are scanning electron microscopy (SEM) images of impact fracture deformation zones (10 μm scale bars for d-f, 5 μm for g), and (h, i) are SEM of tensile fracture deformation zones (5 μm scale bars). The micrographs reveal alternating lamellar structures of soft δ-ferrite and hard carbide/ferrite composite lamellae, with dispersed ultra-fine spherical carbides (<250 nm diameter) in the composite lamellae. Characteristic fracture features are observed: blunted crack tips when penetrating composite lamellae (d), main cracks with secondary horizontal cracks along lamellae (e, g), microvoids/microcracks nucleated at carbide-ferrite interfaces (f), and cracks propagating parallel to lamellar orientation in tensile test samples (h, i). These observations validate that the lamellar structure and dispersed spherical carbides improve toughness by blunting crack tips, dispersing stress concentration, inducing microvoid nucleation to release stress, and extending crack propagation paths, supporting the superior impact toughness and strength-plasticity performance of the R650 sample.",
