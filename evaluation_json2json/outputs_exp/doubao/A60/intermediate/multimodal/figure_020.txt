Three samples are compared: S950 (solution treated at 950 ℃, finest grain size), S1050 (solution treated at 1050 ℃, medium grain size), and S1150 (solution treated at 1150 ℃, coarsest grain size). In the first cycle, an Al oxidation peak appears at ~-0.81 V_SCE, with S950 showing the highest peak current density, indicating higher Al oxidation activity that promotes rapid Al-rich oxide film formation; no Fe/Mn oxidation peak is observed as the formed Al-rich film suppresses Fe/Mn oxidation. In the second cycle, an additional Fe/Mn oxidation peak emerges at ~-0.56 V_SCE, where S950 exhibits the lowest peak intensity, confirming its dense protective Al-rich oxide film inhibits further Fe/Mn oxidation, while S1050 and S1150 show higher peak intensities corresponding to incomplete, less protective surface films. These results support the core conclusion that grain refinement facilitates formation of dense Al-rich oxide film to enhance corrosion resistance of LDSs.",
