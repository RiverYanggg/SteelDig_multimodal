The first image with a 60 μm scale bar corresponds to the Fe–10Al–15Mn–1.0C alloy, the second with a 4.0 μm scale bar corresponds to the Fe–10Al–25Mn–1.0C alloy, and the third with a 1 μm scale bar corresponds to the Fe–10Al–35Mn–1.0C alloy. As Mn content increases from 15 mass% to 35 mass%, the fracture features evolve alongside the reported reduction in ferrite (α) phase fraction and improvement of alloy ductility: the 15Mn alloy presents more brittle fracture features matching its higher strength and lower elongation, while the 35Mn alloy shows finer ductile fracture characteristics consistent with its 27.3% elongation and lower strength.
