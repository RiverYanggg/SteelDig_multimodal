    "description": "This set of 4 scanning electron microscopy (SEM) images characterize the microstructure of Fe-9Al-10Mn-1C lightweight steel after annealing at 800 °C for 30 min followed by water quenching. The first image (1000× magnification, 10 μm scale bar) and second image (5000× magnification, 5 μm scale bar) show regions undergoing spinodal decomposition, with homogeneously distributed nano-sized κ-carbides precipitated in the matrix. The third image (5000× magnification, 5 μm scale bar) and fourth image (10000× magnification, 1 μm scale bar) present the lamellar structure formed via cellular transformation, composed of κ-carbides, α-ferrite and residual austenite. These observations confirm that two distinct phase transformation mechanisms coexist in this alloy at 800 °C: cellular transformation dominates for most austenite fraction, while spinodal decomposition occurs in the remaining austenite, which contributes to the outstanding hardness of this alloy among 9 wt% Al-containing Fe-Mn-Al-C lightweight steels.",
