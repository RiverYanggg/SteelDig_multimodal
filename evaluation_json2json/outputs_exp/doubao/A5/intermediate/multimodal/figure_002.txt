    "description": "This set of optical micrographs (both with 100 μm scale bar) displays the discontinuous precipitation microstructures of two Fe-9Al-10Mn lightweight steels tempered at 600 °C. Subfigure (a) corresponds to the 0.4 wt% C containing alloy (9Al-10Mn-0.4C), where austenite grains only undergo partial discontinuous precipitation, with large volumes of untransformed austenite grains remaining. Subfigure (b) corresponds to the 1.0 wt% C containing alloy (9Al-10Mn-1C), where almost the entire volume of austenite is transformed into γ + κ-carbide lamellar structures, with only a few band-shaped residual austenite regions observed. The comparison demonstrates that higher carbon content significantly promotes the extent of discontinuous precipitation during 600 °C tempering, which enhances the hardness of the steels. This figure supports the conclusion that chemical composition, particularly carbon content, is a critical factor controlling tempering phase transformation behavior and mechanical properties of Fe-Mn-Al-C lightweight steels.",
