    "description": "This figure presents two scanning electron microscopy micrographs showing typical morphologies of AlN inclusions in high-nitrogen (HN) Fe–28Mn–10Al–0.8C low-density steel, with each subplot marked with a 2 μm scale bar. Subplot (a) displays an intact, single polyhedral AlN inclusion with straight edges and sharp angles, representative of as-precipitated AlN in the HN steel matrix. Subplot (b) exhibits a fractured, aggregated AlN inclusion that cracked during the multi-directional hot forging process the steel underwent. AlN is confirmed as the dominant inclusion type in HN steel, where 84% of AlN inclusions are smaller than 2 μm and contribute to austenite grain refinement, while broken aggregated AlN inclusions act as preferred crack initiation sites during material fracture, negatively affecting mechanical properties. This morphology observation supports the correlation between AlN inclusion characteristics, processing history, and mechanical performance of nitrogen-alloyed low-density steels.",
