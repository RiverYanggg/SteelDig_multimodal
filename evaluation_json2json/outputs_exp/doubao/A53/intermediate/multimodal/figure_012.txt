    "description": "This set of scanning electron microscopy (SEM) images presents surface morphologies of oxidized Fe-Mn-Al alloys, with a 20 μm scale bar for both subgraphs. Subgraph (a) shows the surface of Fe-20Mn-8Al alloy after 24 h isothermal oxidation at 800 °C in pure oxygen, where discrete, clustered worm-like manganese oxide threads are distributed on the alumina scale substrate. Subgraph (b) shows the surface of Fe-30Mn-8Al alloy under the same oxidation condition, with much denser, almost continuous manganese oxide threads covering a larger proportion of the surface. The comparison reveals that increasing Mn content from 20 wt% to 30 wt% significantly promotes the formation and coverage of manganese oxide threads, which grow through the preformed alumina scale. These morphologies confirm that higher Mn content and the presence of austenite in the alloy matrix degrade the protectiveness of alumina scales, facilitating the growth of Mn-rich oxides, supporting the core conclusion that austenite is detrimental to the oxidation resistance of duplex Fe-Mn-Al alloys.",
