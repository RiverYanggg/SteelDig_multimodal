    "description": "This group of SEM micrographs shows the microstructural evolution of Fe–10Mn–10Al–0.7C low-density steel after 1 h annealing at temperatures ranging from 700 °C to 1200 °C. Subfigures (a) to (g) display the microstructure of the RD-TD (rolling direction-transverse direction) section of specimens annealed at 700, 800, 850, 900, 950, 1000, and 1100 °C respectively, while subfigure (h) shows the RD-ND (rolling direction-normal direction) section of the specimen annealed at 1200 °C. At 700 °C, γ austenite almost completely decomposes into α ferrite and κ-carbide via eutectoid reaction, with lamellar κ-carbide inside α grains and κ-carbide distributed along α grain boundaries. When annealing temperature rises to 800 °C and 850 °C, κ-carbide coarsens and becomes more globular, its fraction decreases while γ phase fraction increases. From 900 °C to 1100 °C, κ-carbide nearly fully dissolves, only a small amount of κ-carbide precipitates along α grain boundaries during quenching, and grain sizes of both α and γ phases increase apparently with rising temperature. At 1200 °C, grains grow significantly, and acicular/plate-like FCC particles appear in some α grains. These observations reveal the phase transformation and grain growth behavior of the test steel with changing annealing temperature, support the correlation between microstructure and tensile properties, and provide guidance for heat treatment optimization of this low-density steel.",
