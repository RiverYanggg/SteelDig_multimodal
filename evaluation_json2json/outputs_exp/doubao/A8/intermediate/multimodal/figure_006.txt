    "description": "This set of optical micrographs shows the microstructure of Fe–10Mn–10Al–0.7C low-density steel annealed at 800 °C, 850 °C, and 900 °C for 1 h respectively, with a unified scale of 50 μm. Subfigures (a), (c), (e) present microstructures on the rolling direction-normal direction (RD-ND) section, while (b), (d), (f) show microstructures on the rolling direction-transverse direction (RD-TD) section. The banded α-ferrite structure remains across all annealing temperatures. As annealing temperature increases, the fraction of γ-austenite rises, and κ-carbide gradually dissolves: at 800 °C, κ-carbide distributes at α/γ interfaces, α/α grain boundaries, and inside γ grains; at 850 °C, κ-carbide in γ grains disappears and only remains along α/α boundaries; at 900 °C, κ-carbide is almost fully dissolved. These observations verify that the dissolution temperature of κ-carbide in this steel is between 850 °C and 900 °C, and reveal the effect of annealing temperature on phase evolution and morphology of the test steel.",
