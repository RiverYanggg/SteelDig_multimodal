Subfigure (a) shows the hot rolled & quenched (HRQ) specimen, which consists of δ ferrite matrix, untransformed austenite (γ⁰) and martensite (α'). Subfigures (b) to (f) correspond to samples intercritically annealed for 5 min, 30 min, 60 min, 120 min, 180 min, respectively. With increasing annealing time, martensite gradually transforms into needle-shaped reverted austenite (γᵣₑᵥ) and intercritical ferrite (α) at the early stage, then reverted austenite morphology changes to globular shape, its fraction decreases as austenite stabilizing elements (Mn, C) partition to adjacent untransformed austenite, leading to reduced stability of reverted austenite. The sample annealed for 60 min achieves balanced size and composition of reverted austenite, delivering optimum austenite stability and enhanced TRIP effect, while 180 min annealing results in nearly full dissolution of reverted austenite and negligible TRIP effect.",
