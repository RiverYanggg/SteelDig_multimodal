    "description": "This figure presents the flow chart of the VUMAT (User Material Subroutine for Explicit Analysis) in ABAQUS, developed to implement the modified Johnson-Cook constitutive model for Fe-30Mn-9Al-0.8C low-density steel to simulate its plastic flow behavior during 250℃ warm multiaxial forging (MAF). The workflow starts with VUMAT initiation, followed by material constant calculation and step beginning. If step time equals 0, trial stress is calculated under pure elastic assumption; otherwise, deviatoric trial stress difference (sigdif) is computed. When sigdif > 0, stress is updated via the constitutive relation, otherwise equivalent plastic strain is set to zero. Then stress and 6 state-dependent variables (SDVs, including equivalent plastic strain, dislocation density, grain size, etc.) are updated, followed by updating of internal and dissipated inelastic energy before the program ends. This subroutine supports finite element simulation of the MAF process, with simulation results validated against experimental data of MAF-1, MAF-3 and MAF-5 samples.",
