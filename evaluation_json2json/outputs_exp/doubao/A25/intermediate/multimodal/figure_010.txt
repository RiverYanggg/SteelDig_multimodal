    "description": "This figure presents secondary electron SEM images of the 19Al Fe-Mn-Al-Cu low-density steel (nominal composition Fe-34.8Mn-18.6Al-5Cu at.%) after various aging treatments, with aging temperatures ranging from 525 °C to 650 °C and durations of 1 h, 3 h, 6 h, 10 h. Each subgraph uses a 50 μm scale bar, and partial high-magnification insets are equipped with 10 μm scale bars. Characteristic phases including α-Mn, β-Mn, untransformed BCC α matrix, and new fine precipitates are marked and identified in the microstructures. With increasing aging temperature and extended duration, the precipitation and growth of Mn-rich phases and the consumption of initial α matrix are observed. These micrographs confirm that the 19Al alloy has the slowest β-Mn precipitation kinetics among the three investigated Fe-Mn-Al-Cu steels, supporting the conclusion that increased Al content suppresses β-Mn precipitation and shifts the optimal aging temperature for peak hardness to a higher range.",
