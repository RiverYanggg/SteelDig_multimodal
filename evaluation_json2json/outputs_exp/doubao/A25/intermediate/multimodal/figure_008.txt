    "description": "This set of 16 secondary electron scanning electron microscopy (SEM) images presents the microstructure of the 15Al (Fe-35.2Mn-15Al-5.2Cu at.%) low-density steel after aging treatments at 525, 550, 575, 600, 625, and 650 °C for durations of 1 h, 3 h, 6 h, and 10 h, respectively. Each subfigure includes a low-magnification view with a 50 μm scale bar, and most include a high-magnification inset with a 1 μm or 2 μm scale bar. Fine particle-shaped α-Mn precipitates are observed in the early aging stage, while short rod-shaped β-Mn precipitates nucleate along α grain boundaries and inside grains, growing gradually with extended aging time or increased temperature. The α matrix is almost completely transformed to β-Mn after 3 h of aging at 525 °C, and new fine precipitates appear after longer aging or at higher aging temperatures. These observations confirm that β-Mn has rapid precipitation kinetics in the 15Al alloy, and the near-full phase transformation from α matrix to β-Mn is the origin of the achieved ultrahigh hardness of the aged alloy.",
