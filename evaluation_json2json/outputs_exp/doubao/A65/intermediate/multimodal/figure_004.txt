Subfigure (a) shows air induction melted (AIM) Fe-7 wt.% Al alloy, (b) shows flux-covered air induction melted (AIMFC) Fe-7 wt.% Al alloy, (c) shows AIMFC Fe-9 wt.% Al alloy, (d) shows vacuum induction melted (VIM) Fe-7 wt.% Al alloy, and (e) shows VIM Fe-9 wt.% Al alloy, with a 250 μm scale bar provided in subfigure (a). All samples were processed by hot forging and hot rolling (soaked at 1373 K, total thickness reduction of 50%) prior to microstructure observation. All samples exhibit coarse equiaxed single-phase α-Fe(Al) solid solution grains with a small number of dot-like pores or precipitates inside grains. The grain size of AIMFC-produced alloys is comparable to that of VIM-produced counterparts, and Fe-7 wt.% Al alloys have slightly smaller grain sizes than Fe-9 wt.% Al alloys. This figure verifies that the low-cost AIMFC process can achieve grain microstructure equivalent to that of the high-cost VIM process, supporting the feasibility of AIMFC for low-cost manufacturing of lightweight Fe-Al steels.",
