Austenite is concentrated in HR, very scarce in IQ-1050, and widely distributed as fine grains in the tempered martensite matrix of QA specimens. Austenite volume fraction increases with annealing temperature rising from 620 °C to 740 °C, while no obvious increment even slight reduction occurs at 800 °C because partial austenite transforms to martensite during air cooling. This figure confirms that annealing temperature can effectively regulate the content and distribution of austenite to optimize the mechanical properties of the tested δ-TRIP steel.",
