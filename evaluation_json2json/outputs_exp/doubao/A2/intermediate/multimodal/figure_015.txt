The left low-magnification micrograph (scale bar: 0.25 μm) shows overall high-density dislocations distributed across the alloy matrix. The morphologies verify that deformation of this high Al content Fe-Al alloy is dominated by <111> superdislocation motion: high antiphase boundary energy results in small partial dislocation separation, so the observed parallel dislocation pairs are dipoles formed by dislocation reactions rather than glissile superdislocation partials.
