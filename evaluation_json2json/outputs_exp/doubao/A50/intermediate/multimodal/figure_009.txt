    "description": "This figure presents TEM characterization of deformation-induced microstructures in Fe-35Mn-6Al-0.1C low-carbon low-density cryogenic steel under different tensile strains at liquid nitrogen temperature (LNT). Subfigures correspond to strains of 3% (a,b), 10% (c-e), 20% (f-i), 28% (j-o), 38% (p,q), and 55% (r,t). At 3% strain, planar slip features including dislocation pairs, dislocation nodes, and stacking faults at annealed twin boundaries are observed. Deformation twins nucleate at 10% strain with an average twin bundle width of ~5 nm. At 20% strain, dislocation cell blocks and dislocation cells form, with a low fraction of deformation twins. At 28% strain, dislocation substructures are refined, wide microbands (~390 nm width) and fine microbands (~137 nm width) appear, while twin fraction remains low. A large number of deformation twins are activated at 38% strain, and secondary twins with more stacking faults are observed at 55% strain. The figure confirms that deformation twinning initiates at as low as 10% tensile strain at LNT, reveals the evolution of deformation mechanisms from dislocation slip to twinning dominated plasticity with increasing strain, and supports the underlying mechanism for the excellent cryogenic mechanical properties of the studied steel.",
