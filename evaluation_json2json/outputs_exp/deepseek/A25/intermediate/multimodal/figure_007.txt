{"image_type": "diffraction_ebsd", "description": "This figure presents EBSD inverse pole figure (IPF) maps and corresponding phase maps of Fe-35Mn-19Al-5Cu (19Al) low-density steel aged at different temperatures, specifically (a) 525°C for 10 h, (b) 600°C for 3 h, and (c) 650°C for 6 h, with (a₂-c₂) showing magnified views. The IPF maps display crystallographic orientations while phase maps identify four distinct phases: BCC (α-matrix, red), α-Mn (blue), β-Mn (green), and FCC (magenta). At 525°C/10 h (a₁, a₂), the microstructure consists primarily of untransformed α-grains with only 12.1% β-Mn precipitating along grain boundaries as dendritic structures, plus minor α-Mn particles, consistent with slowed precipitation kinetics in high-Al alloys. After aging at 600°C/3 h (b₁, b₂), the matrix has almost completely transformed to β-Mn (green), with small amounts of new FCC phase (magenta) visible at β-Mn interfaces. At 650°C/6 h (c₁, c₂), the α-matrix is fully consumed by β-Mn, and notably, a great amount of new BCC phase (red) precipitates within the β-Mn grains. The scale bars range from 500 μm for overview images to 5-10 μm for magnified views. These observations demonstrate that increasing Al content significantly retards β-Mn precipitation at lower temperatures, while high-temperature aging promotes complete α→β-Mn transformation accompanied by secondary phase formation (FCC and BCC) at β-Mn interfaces or interiors, which affects the aging hardening behavior and fracture toughness.", "confidence": "high"}
