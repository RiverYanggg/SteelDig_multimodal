# Dynamic Recrystallization Mechanisms of an Fe–8% Al Low Density Steel under Hot Rolling Conditions

## Material System
- **Alloy**: Fe–8% Al low-density ferritic steel (nominal composition: Fe-8.15 wt% Al, 0.1 wt% Mn, 0.0086 wt% C, <0.1 wt% total Ti+Nb+V).
- **Phase**: Non-ordered A2 body-centered cubic (BCC) solid solution; fully ferritic across the investigated temperature range (900–1100 °C).
- **Initial condition**: Fully recrystallized equiaxed microstructure with grain size ~800 µm prior to hot torsion deformation.

## Processing Route and Variables
- **Deformation mode**: Hot torsion testing under argon atmosphere to reduce oxidation.
- **Temperature range**: 900, 1000, 1100 °C.
- **Strain rate range**: 0.1, 1, 5, 10 s⁻¹.
- **Accumulated equivalent strain (ε)**: 1, 2, 5, 15, 25.
- **Post-deformation treatment**: Quenching with argon gas, ~0.5 s delay, cooling rate ~200 °C/s.
- **Characterization region**: EBSD observation at 0.9 radius from the torsion axis on the θz plane (near-surface region experiencing highest strain).

## Microstructure and Phase Evolution
- **CDRX regime (lower temperature/strain rate, e.g., 1000 °C, 0.1 s⁻¹)**: Progressive transformation of low-angle subgrain boundaries (LABs) into high-angle grain boundaries (HABs) via continuous subgrain rotation with increasing strain. Original grain boundaries and recrystallized grain boundaries become indistinguishable, forming an aggregate of crystallites bounded by mixed LABs and HABs within elongated original grains.
- **DDRX regime (higher temperature/strain rate, e.g., 1100 °C, 5–10 s⁻¹)**: Small equiaxed, dislocation-free recrystallized grains nucleate along boundaries of elongated parent grains that still contain subgrain networks. Grain structure is heterogeneous, with coexistence of new equiaxed grains and deformed elongated grains containing internal substructures.
  - At 1100 °C, ε = 2: DDRX activated at strain rates of 5 s⁻¹ and 10 s⁻¹ (Figure 4a, b). New DRX grains exhibit random crystallographic orientations rather than retaining the parent deformation texture.
  - Increasing strain rate from 5 s⁻¹ to 10 s⁻¹ promotes DDRX over CDRX.
- **Boundary character transition**:
  - CDRX-dominated microstructure (Figure 5-style): LAB fraction 85%, HAB fraction 15%, average misorientation 9.4°. Distribution dominated by low-angle peak decaying exponentially; reflects progressive subgrain rotation.
  - DDRX-dominated microstructure (Figure 5 conditions: 1100 °C, 5 s⁻¹ or 10 s⁻¹): LAB fraction ~20–22%, HAB fraction ~78–80%, average misorientation 32.9–33.1°. Distribution closely matches the MacKenzie random distribution for cubic crystals, with broad peak near 40–45°.
- **Texture evolution under CDRX** (1000 °C, 0.1 s⁻¹, {110} pole figures, Figure 3):
  - ε = 2–15: Progressive texture sharpening; maximum intensity reaches 3.43 at ε = 15. Strongest component: D2 {-112}<111>, self-symmetric in torsion texture.
  - ε = 25: Texture intensity decreases to 3.17, indicating weakening due to progressive recrystallization.
  - Sharpening up to ε = 15 reflects LAB-to-HAB transformation; subsequent weakening signals CDRX-driven new grain formation.
- **Recrystallized grain orientations under DDRX** (1100 °C, ε = 2, Figure 6):
  - At 5 s⁻¹: Recrystallized grains (internal misorientation <2°) exhibit {110} pole figure maximum intensity 1.77.
  - At 10 s⁻¹: Maximum intensity 1.66.
  - Both near-random orientation distributions, matching the MacKenzie random distribution; minimal variation between the two strain rates.

## Processing-Structure-Property Chain
- **Processing condition → DRX mechanism transition** (Figure 7 map):
  - DDRX activation domain: High temperature (≥900 °C) AND high strain rate (≥1–10 s⁻¹). Specifically: 900 °C/10 s⁻¹, 1000 °C/10 s⁻¹, 1100 °C/10 s⁻¹.
  - CDRX dominant domain: Lower strain rates (0.1 s⁻¹, and 1 s⁻¹ at some temperatures) across 900–1100 °C.
  - Dashed boundary separates DDRX (red) and non-DDRX (green) conditions on strain rate–temperature plot.
- **DRX mechanism → Microstructural output**:
  - CDRX (low ε̇, lower T): Retained sharp deformation texture (D2 component, max intensity 3.43), crystallite aggregate with mixed LABs/HABs, progressive subgrain boundary transformation.
  - DDRX (high ε̇, high T): Near-random textured equiaxed grains (pole figure intensities 1.66–1.77), high HAB fraction (~80%), heterogeneous grain structure with new grains on parent grain boundaries.
- **Microstructural output → Predicted property (roping resistance)**:
  - CDRX produces strong crystallographic texture (D2, intensity up to 3.43), which correlates with high roping tendency in ferritic Fe–Al steels.
  - DDRX produces randomly oriented grains (intensity ~1.7), which is beneficial for minimizing roping defects by suppressing anisotropic deformation during subsequent forming.

## Mechanistic Interpretation
- **CDRX mechanism**: Operates at lower strain rates where dynamic recovery is active. Progressive lattice rotation within subgrains increases subgrain boundary misorientation beyond the 15° threshold, converting LABs into HABs without discrete nucleation events. The original grain structure remains elongated, and texture is inherited and sharpened before eventual weakening at very high strains (ε = 25).
- **DDRX mechanism**: Activated at high temperatures and high strain rates where strain energy accumulation rate exceeds dynamic recovery capability. New dislocation-free grains nucleate at HABs of deformed parent grains and grow via high-angle boundary migration. This nucleation-and-growth process produces randomly oriented equiaxed grains, replacing the deformation texture.
- **Mechanism competition**: The transition between CDRX and DDRX is governed by the Zener–Hollomon parameter (temperature-compensated strain rate). High Z (low T, high ε̇) favors DDRX via sufficient stored energy for nucleation; low Z (high T, low ε̇) favors CDRX via enhanced dynamic recovery that suppresses discrete nucleation.

## Key Quantitative Findings
- **CDRX texture** (1000 °C, 0.1 s⁻¹): Max pole figure intensity 3.43 at ε = 15 (D2 {-112}<111> component); decreases to 3.17 at ε = 25.
- **CDRX boundary distribution**: 85% LABs, 15% HABs, average misorientation 9.4°.
- **DDRX boundary distribution** (1100 °C, ε = 2):
  - Strain rate 5 s⁻¹: 20% LABs, 80% HABs, average misorientation 33.1°.
  - Strain rate 10 s⁻¹: 22% LABs, 78% HABs, average misorientation 32.9°.
- **DDRX texture** (1100 °C, ε = 2): Pole figure maximum intensity 1.77 (5 s⁻¹) and 1.66 (10 s⁻¹), approaching random distribution.
- **DDRX activation conditions**: T ≥ 900 °C, ε̇ ≥ 1–10 s⁻¹ (specifically 900 °C/10 s⁻¹, 1000 °C/10 s⁻¹, 1100 °C/10 s⁻¹).
