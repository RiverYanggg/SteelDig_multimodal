  "abstract_summary": "This study presents a carbon-free, austenite-based low-density triplex steel that incorporates TiB2 particles and δ-ferrite bands to overcome the low Young's modulus and processing difficulties of high-Al Fe-Mn-Al-C steels. The as-hot-rolled heterogeneous microstructure achieves a density of 6.88 g cm−3 and a high Young's modulus of 225 GPa, while nearly doubling the yield strength of conventional Fe-TiB steel without sacrificing ductility. The enhanced strength-ductility balance originates from the composite strengthening of δ-ferrite and the additional strain hardening induced by phase heterogeneity, as evidenced by synchrotron X-ray diffraction and finite element simulations.",
