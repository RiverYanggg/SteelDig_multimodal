{"image_type": "diffraction_xrd", "description": "Figure 1 presents X-ray diffraction (XRD) patterns of Fe-30.34Mn-8.93Al-0.9C low-density steels (LDSs) subjected to different solution treatment conditions. Four curves are displayed: S0 (as-hot-rolled without solution treatment), S950 (solution-treated at 950°C), S1050 (solution-treated at 1050°C), and S1150 (solution-treated at 1150°C), all water-quenched after processing. The diffraction patterns confirm that all samples maintain a single-phase face-centered cubic (FCC) austenitic structure, indexed by the characteristic (111), (200), (220), and (311) reflections. Notably, the relative peak intensities vary systematically with solution treatment temperature: the (111) peak intensity increases significantly while the (220) peak intensity decreases correspondingly as the solution treatment temperature rises. These results indicate that solution treatment preserves the austenitic matrix while modifying the crystallographic texture. The absence of additional peaks confirms the dissolution of secondary phases during high-temperature solution treatment, establishing the foundation for subsequent grain size optimization and corrosion resistance studies.", "confidence": "high"}
