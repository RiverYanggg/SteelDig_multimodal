Subfigure (a) is a bright-field (BF) micrograph revealing the coexistence of three phases: α-ferrite (BCC), γ-austenite (FCC), and κ-carbide (perovskite structure), with the κ-phase appearing as elongated rod-like or globular precipitates distributed along phase boundaries. As reported in the text, examination of multiple areas revealed no orientation relationship between the coarsened κ-carbides and the neighboring α-matrix at this annealing temperature, indicating the loss of coherency that existed at lower temperatures (e.g., Nishiyama–Wasserman relationship at 700 °C).
