Subfigures (a) through (g) display the RD–TD (rolling direction–transverse direction) sections, while (h) shows the RD–ND (rolling direction–normal direction) section. At 700 °C (a), the γ phase has almost completely decomposed into lamellar κ-carbide and α matrix via eutectoid reaction, with continuous κ-carbide along grain boundaries. At 800 °C (b) and 850 °C (c), the κ-carbide coarsens into globular shapes and decreases in fraction, while γ phase fraction increases to approximately 29% and 39%, respectively. From 900 °C (d) to 1100 °C (g), κ-carbide dissolves completely (with only trace boundary precipitation during quenching), resulting in α+γ dual-phase microstructures with progressively increasing grain sizes. At 1200 °C (h), significant grain coarsening occurs (α grains reaching 68 μm), with needle-shaped FCC particles appearing within large α grains. The images demonstrate the dissolution temperature of κ-carbide lies between 850 °C and 900 °C, and reveal the transition from brittle eutectoid microstructure to ductile dual-phase structure as annealing temperature increases.", "confidence": "high"}
