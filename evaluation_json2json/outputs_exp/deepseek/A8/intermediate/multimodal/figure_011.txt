Subfigures (a) through (c) reveal continuous or semi-continuous κ-carbide precipitates (labeled \"boundary κ\") decorating the α-ferrite grain boundaries, with scale bars of 10 μm, 10 μm, and 5 μm respectively. Subfigure (d), at 15 μm scale, distinctly shows boundary κ-carbide alongside needle-shaped or plate-shaped FCC particles precipitated within large α grains. The micrographs demonstrate that boundary κ-carbide persists along α/α boundaries across the entire 900–1200 °C annealing range, forming during quenching when Mn, Al, and C segregate to grain boundaries. At 1200 °C, the intragranular FCC particles compete with boundary κ for Mn and C elements. These images support the conclusion that while κ-carbide dissolves in the matrix below 900 °C, boundary κ remains stable at higher temperatures, critically influencing the microstructural stability and mechanical property evolution of the steel.", "confidence": "high"}
