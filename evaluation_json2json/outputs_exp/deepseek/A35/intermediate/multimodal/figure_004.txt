Dark-field TEM images (a, e, i) with 50 nm scale bars reveal coherent nano-sized κ-carbide particles (3–10 nm, white arrows) uniformly distributed within the austenite matrix across all conditions. Corresponding SAED patterns (b, f, j) display superlattice diffraction spots (red and yellow boxes) confirming the orientation relationship [011]γ//[011]κ between κ-carbide and the austenite matrix. Bright-field TEM images differentiate grain boundary precipitates by treatment: S1000 shows elongated stripe-like Cr7C3 particles at austenite grain boundaries (c, 500 nm scale bar, red circle) with SAED pattern (d) indexed along [11̄2]Cr7C3 zone axis showing (220) and (02̄1) reflections. In S1050 and MHT, Cr7C3 dissolves and B2 phase precipitates form within austenite grains (g and k, red circles) with sizes ranging 20–160 nm; SAED patterns confirm B2 structure along [1̄11] (h) and [113] (l) zone axes respectively. Notably, MHT exhibits slightly smaller B2 particles than S1050. These observations demonstrate the transition from deleterious grain-boundary Cr7C3 to intragranular B2 phase with increasing solution temperature, providing critical microstructural evidence for the 88.6% improvement in impact toughness.", "confidence": "high"}
