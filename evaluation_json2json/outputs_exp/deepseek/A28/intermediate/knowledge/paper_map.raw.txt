  "abstract_summary": "The temperature-dependent deformation and mechanical properties of an Fe–20Mn–6Al-0.6C-0.15Si austenitic low-density steel were investigated via tensile and Charpy impact tests at −196°C, −77°C, and room temperature. Decreasing temperature reduces stacking fault energy, promoting planar dislocation glide and increasing low-angle grain boundary fraction, which improves both tensile strength and elongation.
