At 5% strain (a), unidirectional slips are observed on the (111) plane with 0.5 μm scale bar. As strain increases to 10% (b) and 20% (c), multidirectional slips occur on {111} planes including (111) and (11̄1). Subfigures (e) and (f) illustrate the dislocation substructures in ferrite at 5% and 40% strain with 1 μm scale bars, respectively. At low strain (e), dislocations dominate the deformation mechanism, while at high strain (f), explosive dislocation multiplication develops into dislocation cells. These observations demonstrate that austenite exhibits a distinct planar slip mechanism characteristic of high stacking fault energy, whereas ferrite shows wavy slip characteristics and forms dislocation cells during deformation.", "confidence": "high"}
