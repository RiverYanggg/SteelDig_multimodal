{"image_type": "microscopy_sem", "description": "This figure presents four scanning electron microscopy (SEM) micrographs illustrating the surface deformation morphologies of Fe-30.5Mn-8Al-1C austenitic low-density steel after fatigue testing under different conditions. Subfigures (a) and (b) display the solution-treated (ST) sample fatigued at stress amplitudes (Δσ/2) of 180 MPa and 315 MPa, respectively. At 180 MPa, the ST sample exhibits wavy slip characterized by cross slip events (marked by red arrows), while at 315 MPa, severe strain localization produces kink bands (indicated by green dashed lines). In contrast, subfigures (c) and (d) show the 600-6 sample (aged at 600°C for 6 hours) tested at the same respective stress amplitudes. Both images reveal distinct, uniformly spaced planar slip bands (highlighted by purple dashed lines) intersecting at specific angles. At 315 MPa, the slip band density increases while maintaining uniformity. The comparison demonstrates that intragranular κ-carbide precipitation in the 600-6 sample promotes reversible planar slip and homogeneous strain accommodation, whereas the ST sample undergoes irreversible wavy slip leading to catastrophic strain localization. All micrographs include 5 μm scale bars.", "confidence": "high"}
