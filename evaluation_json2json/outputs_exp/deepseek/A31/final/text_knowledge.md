# Influence of Al Content and Precipitation State on Mechanical Behavior of Austenitic High-Mn Low-Density Steels

## Material System
- **Base alloys**: Two austenitic low-density steels with different Al contents
  - **2Al**: Fe–30.5Mn–2.1Al–1.2C (wt.%)
  - **8Al**: Fe–30.5Mn–8.0Al–1.2C (wt.%)
- **Precipitation phases**:
  - In 2Al after long annealing: intergranular M₃C-type carbides (coarse, incoherent)
  - In 8Al after annealing: nanosized κ-carbides (L'1₂-type ordered phase, shearable)
- Initial microstructure after solution treatment: single-phase austenite (γ) without precipitates.

## Processing Route and Variables
- **Cast and hot-roll**: Ingot reheat at 1200°C, hot-rolled with 75% thickness reduction at 1100°C, water quench.
- **Solution treatment**: 1100°C for 2 h under Ar atmosphere, water quench → fully austenitic, precipitate-free state.
- **Heat treatments (annealing)**:
  - Short annealing for both alloys: 450°C / 1 h
  - Long annealing for 2Al: 650°C / 72 h → intergranular M₃C carbides
  - Long annealing for 8Al: 600°C / 24 h → uniform κ-carbide dispersion
- **Mechanical testing**: Room temperature tensile tests, initial strain rate = 5×10⁻⁴ s⁻¹.
- **Microstructure characterization at defined strains**: Interrupted tests at true strains of 0.05, 0.1, 0.15, 0.2, 0.3 and after fracture. ECCI and TEM performed on longitudinal sections (perpendicular to tensile axis).

## Microstructure and Phase Evolution
### 2Al alloy
- **2Al-450°C/1h (short-annealed)**: Essentially no coarse precipitates; deformation introduces dislocation cell blocks and deformation twins.
- **2Al-650°C/72h (long-annealed)**: Coherent-free M₃C carbides form exclusively at grain boundaries and annealing twin interfaces. Carbide dimensions ~1 μm wide × 3 μm long (Figure 3b). These are incoherent and act as weak interfaces.

### 8Al alloy
- **8Al-450°C/1h (short-annealed)**: Heterogeneous precipitation of shearable nanosized κ-carbides, size 2–5 nm (L'1₂ type). Distribution is non-uniform (Figure 5b).
- **8Al-600°C/24h (long-annealed)**: Uniform dispersion of cuboidal κ-carbides, size 10–20 nm. Dense precipitation results in narrow matrix channels ~2–3 nm wide (Figure 5d).

## Processing–Structure–Property Chain

### 2Al-450°C/1h (short-annealed)
- **Processing → Structure**: Absence of grain boundary carbides after 450°C/1h; clean austenite.
- **Structure → Properties**: High ductility (uniform elongation ≈ 75%), ultimate tensile strength (UTS) ≈ 935 MPa (Figure 1).
- **Deformation stages**: Strain hardening rate shows two-stage behavior (Figure 2). First stage from ≈400 MPa to ≈1000 MPa, second hardening stage onset at ≈1100 MPa, attributed to deformation twinning.
- **Microstructure evolution**: At true strain 0.2, dislocation cell blocks with average size 275 ± 50 nm (Figure 3a). Twinning activated at intermediate stress.
- **Fracture**: Ductile; no premature intergranular cracking.

### 2Al-650°C/72h (long-annealed)
- **Processing → Structure**: Long annealing precipitates coarse intergranular M₃C carbides along grain boundaries and twin interfaces.
- **Structure → Properties**: UTS ≈ 850 MPa, limited uniform elongation ≈ 35% (Figure 1). True fracture stress ≈ 1150 MPa.
- **Strain hardening behavior**: Continuous decrease in hardening rate; no second hardening stage (Figure 2). Fracture coincides with the onset stress of twinning-enhanced hardening observed in the carbide-free counterpart.
- **Deformation–fracture interaction**: Deformation twins impinge on grain boundary carbides (Figure 3c), creating local shear stress concentrations. The estimated twinning stress is 670–750 MPa; failure occurs when twin tip stresses exceed grain boundary cohesion.
- **Fracture mode**: Intergranular fracture; faceted grain surfaces (Figure 3d).

### 8Al-450°C/1h (short-annealed)
- **Processing → Structure**: Heterogeneous 2–5 nm shearable κ-carbides.
- **Structure → Properties**: UTS ≈ 895 MPa, uniform elongation ≈ 65% (Figure 1).
- **Strain hardening**: Three-stage hardening with a plateau region at ≈2 GPa between 800–1100 MPa (Figure 4).
- **Deformation substructure**: At true strain 0.3, planar dislocation configurations (Taylor lattices) are observed (Figure 5a). Planar slip is promoted by shearable precipitates.

### 8Al-600°C/24h (long-annealed)
- **Processing → Structure**: Uniform dispersion of cuboidal κ-carbides (10–20 nm), narrow matrix channels (2–3 nm). High precipitate volume fraction.
- **Structure → Properties**: Yield stress ≈ 990 MPa, UTS ≈ 1180 MPa, uniform elongation ≈ 37% (Figure 1). Highest strength among all conditions with good ductility.
- **Strain hardening**: Initial high hardening rate (≈5 GPa), sharp drop to ≈2 GPa at ≈1150 MPa, followed by a distinct upward inflection to ≈2.2 GPa at stresses >1400 MPa (Figure 4).
- **Deformation substructure**: At true strain 0.15, dense planar dislocation arrays spaced 100–300 nm (Figure 5c). Shearable κ-carbides are cut by dislocations (Figure 5d), stabilizing planar slip and promoting sustained work hardening at high stresses.

## Mechanistic Interpretation
- **2Al alloy**: The absence of grain boundary carbides (short anneal) allows full exploitation of twinning-induced hardening. When coarse M₃C carbides are present, twin tips generate local shear concentrations at particle–matrix interfaces, triggering intergranular fracture before the bulk hardening potential is realized.
- **8Al alloy**: κ-carbides are shearable. Their size and distribution dictate slip planarity and work hardening. Heterogeneous, fine precipitates (2–5 nm) provide moderate planar slip contribution. Uniform, coarser cuboidal precipitates (10–20 nm) with narrow channels force dislocations into highly confined planar arrays; shearing of these precipitates continuously regenerates hardening capacity, enabling the unusual hardening rate up-turn and delivering an excellent strength–ductility combination.
- **Relationship between precipitate state and hardening**: In 8Al long-annealed, the combination of high initial yield strength from precipitation and sustained planar-slip-based strain hardening results from the high volume fraction of shearable cuboidal κ-carbides, with matrix channel dimensions on the order of a few nanometers.

## Key Quantitative Findings
- **Mechanical properties (engineering, room temperature, 5×10⁻⁴ s⁻¹)**:
  - 2Al-450°C/1h: UTS ≈ 935 MPa, UE ≈ 75%
  - 2Al-650°C/72h: UTS ≈ 850 MPa, UE ≈ 35%; true fracture stress ≈ 1150 MPa
  - 8Al-450°C/1h: UTS ≈ 895 MPa, UE ≈ 65%
  - 8Al-600°C/24h: YS ≈ 990 MPa, UTS ≈ 1180 MPa, UE ≈ 37%
- **Microstructural dimensions**:
  - 2Al-450°C/1h, strain 0.2: dislocation cell blocks 275 ± 50 nm
  - M₃C carbides: ~1 μm × 3 μm
  - κ-carbide sizes: short-annealed 2–5 nm; long-annealed 10–20 nm
  - Matrix channels in 8Al long-annealed: ~2–3 nm
  - Planar slip band spacing in 8Al long-annealed: 100–300 nm
- **Estimated twinning stress in 2Al**: 670–750 MPa

## Visual Evidence
- **Figure 1**: Engineering stress–strain curves of the four annealed conditions. Directly shows that long-annealed 8Al achieves highest strength (YS 990 MPa, UTS 1180 MPa) with 37% elongation, short-annealed 2Al exhibits maximum ductility (75%), and long-annealed 2Al is severely embrittled (35% elongation).
- **Figure 2**: Strain hardening rate vs. true stress for 2Al alloys. Confirms that the short-annealed condition displays a two-stage hardening response with a second regime emerging near 1100 MPa, while the long-annealed condition fractures at the same stress level without any second hardening, supporting twin–carbide interaction as the embrittlement mechanism.
- **Figure 3**: Deformation and fracture micrographs of long-annealed 2Al. (a) Dislocation cells at 0.2 strain. (b) M₃C carbides at grain boundaries. (c) Twin bundles impinging on carbides at fracture. (d) Intergranular fracture surface. Provides direct evidence that twin impingement on grain boundary carbides triggers intergranular failure.
- **Figure 4**: Strain hardening rate curves for 8Al alloys. Demonstrates the upward inflection in work hardening rate at high stress in the long-annealed state, absent in the short-annealed material. This correlates with the uniform dispersion of cuboidal κ-carbides enabling sustained planar-slip hardening.
- **Figure 5**: ECCI and dark-field TEM of 8Al alloys. (a) Taylor lattice in short-annealed. (b) 2–5 nm κ-carbides. (c) Dense planar slip arrays (100–300 nm spacing) in long-annealed. (d) Sheared 10–20 nm cuboidal κ-carbides and narrow matrix channels. Links precipitate distribution and slip character to macroscopic hardening behavior.
