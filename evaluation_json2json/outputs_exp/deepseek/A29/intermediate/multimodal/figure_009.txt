Panel (a) displays the evolution of diffraction peaks at true strains of 0.14, 0.22, 0.3, and 0.38 for both rolling direction (RD, red curves) and transverse direction (TD, blue curves) samples. The FCC peaks (γ311, γ222, marked by orange diamonds) corresponding to austenite decrease in intensity with increasing strain, while BCC/BCT peaks (α211, α220, marked by green circles) corresponding to ferrite/martensite increase, indicating strain-induced martensitic transformation. Notably, BCC peak intensities are higher in RD samples than TD samples at high strains (ε>0.22), suggesting enhanced martensite formation in RD. Panel (b) quantifies the martensite fraction calculated by the Rietveld refinement method as a function of true strain, showing that RD exhibits faster transformation kinetics with a difference of up to 4 vol.% at ε≈0.48 compared to TD. The inset displays the corresponding strain hardening rate curves, confirming higher work hardening capability in RD. These results demonstrate that anisotropic strain partitioning between banded δ-ferrite and ultrafine-grained γ+α aggregates leads to different austenite strain levels, governing the TRIP effect and mechanical anisotropy.", "confidence": "high"}
