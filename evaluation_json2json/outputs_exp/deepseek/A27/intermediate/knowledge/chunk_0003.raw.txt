The discussion then addresses short-range ordering: it occurs in 13–18% Al alloys below 600 °C, is maximal after 250 °C/14 days (Fe-18%Al) with an activation energy of 0.3 eV, and is suppressed by 1100 °C quenching. The dominant factor is quenched-in excess vacancies: their formation enthalpy (1–1.2 eV for 16–18% Al) leads to high concentrations that increase with Al content, pin dislocations, and produce the highest hardness after quenching.
