Panels (a), (c), and (e) are bright-field (BF) images showing the γ-austenite matrix with retained dislocation slip bands. Selected area diffraction patterns (SADPs) in the insets along [1̄10] zone axis confirm the fcc structure with (001) superlattice reflections (circled in red) indicating ordered κ-carbides. Panels (b), (d), and (f) are dark-field (DF) images obtained using the (001) superlattice reflection to visualize κ-carbides. In the 0Cr and 3Cr steels, κ-carbides (marked K with yellow arrows) are clearly visible and mainly precipitate at dislocation entanglements. However, in the 6Cr steel, κ-carbides are too fine to be resolved in the DF image (f). These micrographs demonstrate that post-deformation annealing promotes κ-carbide precipitation but does not eliminate pre-existing deformation structures, explaining the limited yield strength increase despite precipitation.", "confidence": "high"}
