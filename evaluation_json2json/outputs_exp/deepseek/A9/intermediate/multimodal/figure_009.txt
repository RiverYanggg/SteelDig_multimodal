Panel (a) shows the CR20% condition, while panel (b) displays the annealed state. The patterns reveal coexistence of γ-austenite (fcc, red indices), δ-ferrite (bcc, blue indices), and ordered DO3 phase. Notably, in the 6Cr sample of panel (a), distinct (110)δ and (220)DO3 peaks are visible (marked by blue arrows), indicating separated ferrite and ordered phase diffraction. After annealing (panel b), these peaks evolve into a merged (110)δ/(220)DO3 profile, suggesting enhanced atomic ordering. The spectra confirm that increasing Cr content promotes δ-ferrite formation (volume fraction increasing from ~5% to 18%) and influences nano-scale κ-carbide precipitation, which occurs primarily at dislocation entanglements in the annealed condition.", "confidence": "high"}
