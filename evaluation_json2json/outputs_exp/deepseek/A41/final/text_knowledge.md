# Material System
Five low-density nano-bainitic Al–C steels were investigated, designated S1–S5. The base system is Fe‑C‑Al‑Si‑Mn‑Mo‑(B), with Ti and Ni added to selected grades.
- **S1**: 0.98 wt% C, 6.00 wt% Al, B‑microalloyed. High‑C, high‑Al.
- **S2**: Composition analogous to S1 (high‑C, high‑Al, B‑microalloyed).
- **S3**: 1.10 wt% C (?), 8.08 wt% Al, B‑microalloyed. Highest Al content.
- **S4**: 0.87 wt% C, 5.76 wt% Al, 0.10 wt% Ti, 0.48 wt% Mo, 0.78 wt% Ni. Ti‑microalloyed variant; Ni added to suppress polygonal ferrite.
- **S5**: 0.60 wt% C, 3.92 wt% Al, 0.78 wt% Ni. Lowest C and Al; Ni‑alloyed.

Key phases encountered across the alloys: bainitic ferrite (αb), retained austenite (γr), polygonal ferrite (αp), martensite (M), (Fe,M)xCy carbides, TiMoC precipitates, and AlN.

# Processing Route and Variables
Heat treatments were designed to achieve nano‑bainitic microstructures while avoiding martensite and polygonal ferrite.
- **Austenitizing**:
  - S1, S2, S5: 1150 °C / 15 min.
  - S3: 1220 °C / 20 min (to reduce polygonal ferrite; ~8.2% αp persists).
  - S4: 1150 °C / 15 min or 1250 °C / 15 min (plus an intermediate hold at 1150 °C for 900 s after 1250 °C when used for precipitation design).
- **Cooling to austempering**: Rapid cooling at 100 K s⁻¹.
- **Austempering temperatures (T_B)** selected above the respective Ms:
  - S1, S2: 300 °C or 350 °C.
  - S3: 270 °C or 350 °C.
  - S4: 320 °C or 350 °C (depending on T_A).
  - S5: 335 °C or 365 °C.
- **Austempering holding time**: Determined by dilatometric bainite‑finish time (Bt); varied from ~700 s (S5‑335 °C) to ~43 000 s (S3‑270 °C).

# Microstructure and Phase Evolution
- **Prior‑austenite state**: At the selected T_A, polygonal ferrite is eliminated in S1, S2, and largely in S4 and S5 (<1% trace). S3 retains ~8.2% αp even at 1220 °C due to its 8.08 wt% Al.
- **Bainitic transformation products**: Nano‑scale bainitic ferrite laths (true thickness 34–68 nm) interspersed with retained austenite (γr) in film‑like and blocky morphologies.
  - S1, S2 (αp‑free): Vγ = 27–34%, depending on T_B.
  - S3: bainite coexists with ~8.2% polygonal ferrite; sluggish transformation at 270 °C.
  - S4: TiMoC precipitates aligned longitudinally (from hot rolling); they act as nucleation sites but also cause sheaf impingement.
  - S5 at 365 °C: nano‑bainitic lamellae + film‑γr + trace allotriomorphic ferrite; Vγ ~26%. At 335 °C: finer laths (~41 nm vs ~55 nm at 365 °C), Vγ ~19%, and scattered lenticular martensite islands.
- **Precipitation**:
  - (Fe,M)xCy forms in S1, S2, S3, and S5 during austempering, located at phase boundaries.
  - In S4, thermodynamically stable TiMoC suppresses (Fe,M)xCy and κ‑carbide; AlN particles also observed.
- **Martensite**: In S5 austempered at 335 °C, stress‑assisted lenticular martensite appears embedded in bainitic lamellae, absent at 365 °C.

# Processing‑Structure‑Property Chain
1. **Alloy composition → Ms → T_B selection → transformation kinetics**:
   - Highest Al (S3, 8.08 wt%) → lowest Ms (258 °C), requires low T_B (270 °C) → extremely sluggish kinetics (Bt up to 43 000 s). Even at 350 °C, Bt = 14 000 s.
   - Moderate C/Al (S5) → Ms = 331 °C, allowing T_B = 335–365 °C → fastest kinetics (Bt ~700–1000 s).
   - Ti‑microalloyed S4 → Ms = 305–310 °C; TiMoC accelerates nucleation but impingement slows overall progress; Bt = 2600–3000 s.
2. **Kinetics + initial αp → bainite morphology → Vγ and carbide distribution**:
   - Rapid transformation (S5) → fine connected bainitic networks, Vγ 19–26%, absence of blocky γr, and (Fe,M)xCy at boundaries.
   - Slow transformation (S1, S2) → co‑existing blocky and film γr, Vγ 27–34%, and pronounced carbide precipitation.
   - Persistent αp in S3 dilutes the bainitic fraction and impairs ductility.
   - TiMoC in S4 suppresses (Fe,M)xCy, preserving more γr for TRIP effect.
3. **Microstructure → mechanical properties**:
   - **S4 (1150 °C / 350 °C)** achieves the best strength–ductility balance: UTS ≈ 1360 MPa, total elongation (El) ~10–12%, supported by nano‑bainitic laths, retained austenite, and TiMoC‑suppressed carbide precipitation.
   - **S5 (365 °C)**: UTS = 1315 MPa, El = 8.7%; (335 °C): UTS rises to 1530 MPa, El drops to 7% due to finer laths + stress‑induced martensite.
   - **S1, S2** at 300–350 °C: UTS up to ~1600 MPa, moderate El (exact values not provided for S3 at 350 °C, but S3‑270 °C shows severely degraded ductility ~2.4%).
   - **Hardness** ranges from 443 HV30 to 500 HV30 across all conditions.
4. **Role of T_B**: Lower T_B universally increases strength and hardness but reduces ductility, primarily by refining lath thickness and, in S5, introducing martensite.

# Mechanistic Interpretation
- **Al content effect**: Al raises the Ae3 temperature, stabilizes ferrite, and increases the temperature required for complete carbide dissolution. This demands a higher T_A; otherwise, persistent polygonal ferrite degrades properties. High Al also enriches austenite in carbon, depressing Ms and slowing bainitic transformation.
- **Ti‑microalloying effect (S4)**: TiMoC precipitates, formed during post‑casting cooling and aligned by hot rolling, provide heterogeneous nucleation sites for bainitic ferrite, accelerating the early stage of transformation. However, the high density of phase boundaries causes early impingement of bainite sheaves, slowing the later stage. Thermodynamically, TiMoC is more stable than (Fe,M)xCy, thereby inhibiting embrittling carbide precipitation and promoting retained austenite stability, which enhances TRIP‑mediated ductility.
- **C + Al balance (S5)**: Lower C and Al result in higher Ms and faster transformation, yielding finely interconnected bainite laths. The moderate retained austenite fraction (19–26%) undergoes stress‑induced martensitic transformation (TRIP), improving work hardening. Excessive refinement at 335 °C causes too much martensite formation and early failure.
- **Martensite formation**: In S5 at 335 °C, insufficient thermal stabilization of austenite during rapid bainitic transformation leads to stress‑assisted martensite upon final cooling or deformation, reducing ductility.

# Key Quantitative Findings
- **Transformation finish times**: S3‑270 °C: 43 000 s; S3‑350 °C: 14 000 s; S1‑350 °C: 2300 s; S1‑300 °C: 3600 s; S4‑350 °C: 2600–3000 s; S5‑365 °C: 1000 s; S5‑335 °C: 700 s.
- **Ms temperatures**: S1 = 277 °C, S2 = 274 °C, S3 = 258 °C, S4 = 305–310 °C (T_A‑dependent), S5 = 331 °C.
- **Bainitic lath true thickness**: 34–68 nm across all steels; S5 refines from ~55 nm (365 °C) to ~41 nm (335 °C).
- **Retained austenite volume fraction (Vγ)**: S1/S2 = 27–34%; S5 = 19% (335 °C) / 26% (365 °C).
- **Tensile properties**:
  - UTS range: 1315–1600 MPa.
  - Total elongation (El): S4‑1150 °C/350 °C ≈ 10–12%; S3‑270 °C ≈ 2.4%; S5‑335 °C = 7%; S5‑365 °C = 8.7%.
  - Hardness: 443–500 HV30.
