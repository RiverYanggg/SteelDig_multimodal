Image (b) is a dark-field TEM image captured using the superlattice reflection (210) of the [001] fcc zone axis, revealing a regular, periodic arrangement of nanosized j-carbides (κ-carbides) with dimensions of 20–30 nm that maintain coherency with the austenitic matrix. The images collectively demonstrate that the uniform distribution of coherent nanoscale κ-carbides gives rise to homogeneously arranged shear bands, which is attributed to the highly stable austenite possessing high positive Gibbs energy (1755 J mol⁻¹) for austenite-to-martensite transformation and relatively high stacking fault energy (110 mJ m⁻²).
