The AC-annealed samples exhibit duplex microstructures comprising needle-like α-ferrite embedded within austenite bands and δ-ferrite, with the needle-like ferrite refining austenite grains (average sizes 3.5–5.8 μm). These microstructural differences demonstrate that the AC path enhances austenite stability through grain refinement by needle-like α-ferrite, enabling transformation-induced plasticity and superior ductility, whereas the FC path introduces brittle martensite and banded structures that promote crack nucleation at martensite-austenite interfaces.", "confidence": "high"}
