{"image_type": "microscopy_tem", "description": "This figure presents a pair of bright-field transmission electron microscopy (TEM) micrographs of deformed Fe–28% Al alloy (the composition corresponding to the peak in the work-hardening curve) imaged under +g and −g conditions. The micrographs reveal dislocation substructures consisting predominantly of superdislocations composed of two (1/2)<111> partials. The images specifically highlight two distinct configurations: dipole pairs (marked \"d\") and non-dipole dislocation pairs (marked \"p\"). The dipole configurations are confirmed by the change in relative dislocation positions when the sign of the imaging vector g (±200, arrowed) is reversed, with the zone axis near [001]. The non-dipole pairs exhibit identical Burgers vectors. These observations demonstrate that at 28% Al, dislocations are invariably superdislocations trailing APBII faults, mostly oriented in near-screw directions along {110} glide planes. The high density of superdislocation dipoles and the predominance of straight screw dislocations, combined with the formation of sessile <100> edge dislocation locks via reactions between superdislocations with different <111> Burgers vectors, provide the microscopic basis for the anomalous peak in work hardening observed in this alloy composition.", "confidence": "high"}
