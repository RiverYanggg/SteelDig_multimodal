Subfigures (a)-(d) display bright-field (BF) images and corresponding selected-area diffraction patterns (SADPs) for 0Cr, 3Cr, 6Cr, and 9Cr strips, respectively. The micrographs reveal that dislocation density in austenite increases with Cr addition: few dislocations are visible in 0Cr, while progressively higher densities appear in 3Cr and 6Cr, with 9Cr exhibiting high but slightly lower density than 6Cr. SADPs show clear superlattice spots attributed to coherent κ-carbides in 0Cr, very weak superlattices in 3Cr and 6Cr, and complete absence of κ-carbide superlattice in 9Cr, demonstrating that Cr addition suppresses κ-carbide precipitation. Subfigure (e) is a dark-field (DF) image of 0Cr using (010)κ reflection, revealing nano-sized κ-carbides at austenite-ferrite phase boundaries. Subfigure (f) shows Cr23C6 carbides at austenite grain boundaries in 9Cr, identified as cubic structure with lattice constant 1.054 nm and width of approximately 100 nm. These observations collectively indicate that Cr addition increases dislocation density while inhibiting κ-carbide formation, and promotes Cr23C6 precipitation at grain boundaries in high-Cr alloys.", "confidence": "high"}
