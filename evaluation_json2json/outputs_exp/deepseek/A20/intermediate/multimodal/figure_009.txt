Each subplot displays elastic modulus values (in GPa) as a function of position (in micrometers), with a marked \"Grain boundary\" indicated by an arrow. The data reveal a distinct trend where the elastic modulus drops significantly near the grain boundary (approximately 50-150 GPa) compared to the grain interior regions (ranging from 200 to 350 GPa). The three plots represent measurements along three different scan lines across the boundary, showing similar qualitative behavior but with varying baseline modulus levels attributed to different grain orientations. The results demonstrate that the elastic modulus near grain boundaries has smaller values than in other regions, supporting the dislocation pile-up model where movable dislocations accumulate near boundaries during plastic deformation, causing an apparent reduction in local elastic properties.
