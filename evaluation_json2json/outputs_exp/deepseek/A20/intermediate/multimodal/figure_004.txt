{"image_type": "property_mechanical", "description": "This figure presents the variation in Young's modulus within the elastic region for pre-strained SPCC steel specimens. Subfigure (a) displays stress–strain curves measured by strain gauge for five conditions: A (as-received SPCC), B (15% tensile pre-strain), C (15% pre-strain with subsequent heat treatment at 373 K for 90 h), D (as-received with heat treatment), and E (annealed). The curves reveal that pre-strained specimens (B and C) exhibit nonlinear behavior with decreasing slope even within the apparent elastic region, while the annealed specimen (E) maintains linearity. Subfigure (b) plots the tangent elastic modulus (gradient of stress–strain curves) against strain, showing that pre-strained conditions (A, B, C) experience a drastic reduction from initial values of ~200–240 GPa down to near zero at 0.3% strain, whereas the annealed specimen (E) retains a nearly constant modulus of ~200 GPa. These results support the conclusion that movable dislocations, released from Cottrell atmospheres during plastic deformation and pinned at grain boundaries, cause the apparent reduction in elastic modulus, which can be partially recovered through appropriate heat treatment.", "confidence": "high"}
