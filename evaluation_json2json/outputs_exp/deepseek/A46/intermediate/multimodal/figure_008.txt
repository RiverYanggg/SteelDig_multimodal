These images reveal the evolution of fracture morphology as the macroscopic microstructure transitions from duplex austenite (γ) and ferrite (α/D03) phases toward a predominantly austenitic structure with increasing Mn addition. At 15% Mn, the fracture surface exhibits coarse features characteristic of the two-phase mixture containing approximately 33% α-phase. As Mn increases to 25%, the ferrite content decreases to about 15.7%, resulting in finer fracture characteristics, while the 35% Mn alloy shows fine-scale fracture morphology consistent with the nearly single γ-phase structure containing only ~2.6% α-phase. The micrographs illustrate how the reduction in D03 ordered ferrite and the distribution of κ-carbide precipitates (κ′, κ, and κ′′) influence the fracture mechanisms and corresponding mechanical properties, including ultimate tensile strength ranging from 646–887 MPa and elongation of 16.3–29.2%.", "confidence": "high"}
