# Decomposition in Fe–Mn–Al–C Lightweight Steels

## Material System
- **Alloy family:** Fe–Mn–Al–C lightweight steels.
- **Composition range (wt%):** Al 9–12, Mn 10–30, C 0.4–1.0, balance Fe.
- **Key phases identified:** γ-austenite (FCC_A1), δ/α-ferrite (BCC_A2), κ-carbide (KAPPA_E21, MxCy), ordered BCC (BCC_2SL), residual austenite, and transformation products (lamellar colonies, nano-precipitates).
- **Specific alloys investigated:** 9Al-10Mn-0.4C, 9Al-10Mn-1C, 12Al-10Mn-0.4C, 12Al-10Mn-1C, 9Al-20Mn-1C, 9Al-30Mn-1C, 12Al-20Mn-1C, 12Al-30Mn-1C.

## Processing Route and Variables
- **Initial condition:** Hot-rolled.
- **Primary thermal treatment:** Tempering (isothermal holding).
- **Tempering temperatures:** 600 °C and 800 °C.
- **Tempering time:** 30 minutes (stated for most conditions in figures).
- **Alloy compositional variables:** Al content (9 vs. 12 wt%), Mn content (10 vs. 20 vs. 30 wt%), C content (0.4 vs. 1.0 wt%).

## Microstructure and Phase Evolution
- **Discontinuous precipitation (600 °C):**
  - In 9Al-10Mn-1C, nearly complete austenite decomposition occurs, producing fine γ + κ lamellae nucleated at grain boundaries, with only minimal residual band-shaped γ; 9Al-10Mn-0.4C shows only partial transformation with distinct untransformed γ bands remaining.
  - In 12Al-10Mn-1C, a “precipitation transformation” (complex random-like transformation rather than classical discontinuous precipitation) yields alternating bands of carbon-depleted austenite with globular κ-carbides and γ + κ lamellae; no classical fine lamellar structure.

- **Cellular transformation vs. spinodal decomposition (800 °C):**
  - 9Al-10Mn-1C: mixed microstructure of cellular transformation products (κ + α-ferrite + residual γ lamellae) coexisting with spinodally decomposed regions containing nano-sized acicular κ-carbide agglomerates.
  - 12Al-10Mn-1C: complete cellular transformation into coarse α + κ lamellae with wide inter-lamellar spacing and scattered δ-ferrite bands.
  - 12Al-20Mn-1C: hybrid structure — some γ grains undergo cellular transformation into wide, thick lath-like κ-carbide lamellae, while others undergo spinodal decomposition with dense cuboidal nano-κ carbides.
  - 12Al-30Mn-1C: fully spinodal-decomposed austenite with fine, acicular nano-κ-carbide agglomerates of lowest density.
  - Increasing Mn from 10 to 30 wt% progressively suppresses cellular transformation and promotes spinodal decomposition; spinodal precipitate morphology evolves from acicular agglomerates (10Mn) to dense cuboidal particles (20Mn) and back to finer, less dense acicular precipitates (30Mn).

- **High-Mn alloys at 600 °C:**
  - 9Al-20Mn-1C and 9Al-30Mn-1C show only grain-boundary precipitates (κ and/or γ-carbides; in 20Mn possibly γ+α with continuity to δ-ferrite) without significant bulk decomposition; both exhibit striated austenite grains; hardness unchanged relative to hot-rolled condition.

## Processing-Structure-Property Chain
- **C content effect at 600 °C (9Al-10Mn):**
  - C 0.4 wt% → partial discontinuous precipitation → lower hardness.
  - C 1.0 wt% → nearly complete discontinuous precipitation with fine γ+κ lamellae (short inter-lamellar spacing) → peak hardness **439 HV**.

- **Al content effect at 600 °C (10Mn-1C):**
  - Al 9 wt% (9Al-10Mn-1C) → fine γ+κ lamellae via discontinuous precipitation → **439 HV**.
  - Al 12 wt% (12Al-10Mn-1C) → precipitation transformation (γ+coarse κ bands) → **420 HV**.

- **Temperature effect on 9Al-10Mn-1C:**
  - 600 °C → fine γ+κ lamellae (discontinuous precipitation) → **439 HV**.
  - 800 °C → mixed cellular transformation (coarse κ+α+γ lamellae) + spinodal decomposition (nano-sized acicular κ) → moderate hardness **356 HV**.

- **Mn content effect at 800 °C (12Al-1C series):**
  - 12Al-10Mn-1C → complete cellular transformation, coarse α+κ lamellae → **399 HV**.
  - 12Al-30Mn-1C → complete spinodal decomposition, fine acicular nano-κ → hardness not explicitly linked in core facts.

- **High-Mn 9Al series at 600 °C vs. 800 °C:**
  - 9Al-20Mn-1C and 9Al-30Mn-1C at 600 °C: only grain-boundary precipitates, hardness identical to hot-rolled condition (no hardening).
  - 9Al-20Mn-1C at 800 °C: cellular transformation (κ+α+γ lamellae) + spinodal decomposition; 12Al-20Mn-1C at 800 °C yields **404 HV**.

- **Structural dependency of hardening:** Fine γ+κ discontinuous lamellae at 600 °C produce the highest hardness (439 HV); coarser cellular lamellae at 800 °C yield intermediate hardness (356–420 HV); purely spinodal nano-precipitates and grain-boundary-only precipitates produce lower strengthening.

## Mechanistic Interpretation
- **Thermodynamic driving force:** Higher C (1.0 vs. 0.4 wt%) enhances the chemical driving force for κ-carbide precipitation, increasing the extent of discontinuous precipitation. Higher Al (12 vs. 9 wt%) increases thermodynamic gradients during tempering, promoting austenite decomposition involving the full γ volume.
- **Austenite stability control:** Mn stabilizes γ-austenite against decomposition. At 600 °C, 20–30 wt% Mn suppresses bulk decomposition (only grain-boundary precipitation). At 800 °C, increasing Mn shifts the dominant mechanism from cellular transformation (10Mn) to spinodal decomposition (20–30Mn).
- **Phase fraction gradients (Thermo-Calc evidence, 9Al-10Mn series):**
  - 9Al-10Mn-0.4C: BCC_A2 dominates at low T (~0.9 mol fraction); FCC_A1 rises sharply above ~700 °C, consistent with austenite decomposition at 600 °C but not 800 °C.
  - 9Al-10Mn-1C: FCC_A1 dominates at low T (~0.8 mol fraction), providing driving force for decomposition at both 600 °C and 800 °C.
- **Kinetic competition:** At 800 °C, cellular transformation and spinodal decomposition compete. Cellular transformation dominates in lower-Mn, lower-austenite-stability alloys, while spinodal decomposition prevails in higher-Mn grades with higher austenite stability.

## Key Quantitative Findings
- **Peak hardness:** **439 HV** for 9Al-10Mn-1C tempered at 600 °C (discontinuous precipitation, fine γ+κ lamellae).
- **Hardness after precipitation transformation at 600 °C:** **420 HV** for 12Al-10Mn-1C.
- **Hardness after cellular transformation at 800 °C:** **399 HV** for 12Al-10Mn-1C; **356 HV** for 9Al-10Mn-1C.
- **Hardness after mixed transformation at 800 °C:** **404 HV** for 12Al-20Mn-1C.
- **9Al-20Mn-1C at 600 °C:** hardness identical to hot-rolled condition (no hardening from grain-boundary κ/γ-carbides).
- **Inter-lamellar spacing trend:** Coarser lamellae and wider spacing at higher Al (12 vs. 9 wt%), correlating with lower hardness for cellular products.
