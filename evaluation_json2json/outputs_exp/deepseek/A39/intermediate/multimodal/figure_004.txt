Subfigure (a) shows the M1-SS sample (Ti/V-free steel) exhibiting a clean austenitic matrix devoid of visible precipitates, confirming the single-phase solid solution state. Subfigure (b) displays the M2-SS sample (Ti/V-modified steel containing 0.62 wt% V and 0.09 wt% Ti), where dark contrast particles identified as (Ti,V)C carbides are dispersed throughout the matrix (indicated by blue arrows). According to the accompanying text, these carbides have an average size of 35.8 ± 20.1 nm and precipitate during high-temperature solution treatment. The comparison demonstrates that Ti/V additions lead to formation of (Ti,V)C carbides that act as carbon sinks, reducing matrix carbon content and thereby altering the precipitation behavior of κ-carbides during subsequent aging.
