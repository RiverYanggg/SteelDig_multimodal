Subfigures (a–c) are OM images showing the lamellar microstructure with alternating δ-ferrite and carbide/ferrite composite lamellae, where cracks initiate and propagate. Subfigures (d–g) are SEM images of the impact fracture zone: (d) shows a significantly blunted crack tip (black arrow) arrested at carbide/ferrite lamellae; (e) identifies primary and secondary cracks; (f) reveals microvoids and microcracks (white arrows) nucleated at carbide/ferrite interfaces; and (g) displays interface separation (dashed lines) along the lamellae. Subfigures (h,i) are SEM images of the tensile fracture zone, demonstrating cracks confined within and propagating parallel to the δ-ferrite lamellae. The figure evidences that nanoscale spherical carbides (diameter <250 nm) dispersed in ferrite lamellae effectively blunt crack tips, increase the curvature radius to reduce stress concentration, and induce microvoid formation at interfaces, thereby deflecting cracks horizontally along lamellae rather than allowing vertical penetration. This crack arrest mechanism explains the superior impact toughness (207.5 J) and strength-plasticity synergy in the low-temperature rolled microstructure compared to high-temperature rolled counterparts.", "confidence": "high"}
