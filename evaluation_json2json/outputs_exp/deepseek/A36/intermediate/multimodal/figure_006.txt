Each panel displays high-magnification views of the fibrous fracture zone alongside insets showing the macroscopic fracture surface morphology. All four steels exhibit characteristic dimple patterns across their fracture surfaces, indicating ductile fracture modes regardless of specific alloying combinations. The micrographs reveal numerous equiaxed dimples of varying sizes and depths distributed throughout the fibrous zones, consistent with microvoid coalescence mechanisms. While the 2Ni steel shows relatively finer and shallower dimples correlating with its lower elongation of 34.1%, the 3Cr, 2Ni3Cr, and 2Ni5Cr steels display deeper, more developed dimple structures corresponding to their superior ductility ranging from 45.9% to 54.4% elongation. These fractographic observations demonstrate that the synergy of Ni and Cr alloying successfully maintains ductile fracture behavior despite significant precipitation strengthening from nanoscale κ-carbides or long-range ordered domains.", "confidence": "high"}
