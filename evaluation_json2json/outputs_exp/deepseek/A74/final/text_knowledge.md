## Material System
- Base alloy: Fe-15Mn-C low-density steel with three nominal Al levels (x = 1, 3, 5 wt% Al), denoted as samples 1# (Fe-15Mn-1Al-C), 2# (Fe-15Mn-3Al-C) and 3# (Fe-15Mn-5Al-C).
- Measured Al contents: 0.87 wt% (1#), 2.77 wt% (2#), 4.70 wt% (3#); C ≈ 0.4 wt%; Mn ≈ 15 wt%; Ti, N, S, O present at impurity/tramp levels.
- Matrix phases: austenite (FCC) and ferrite (BCC), with phase balance depending on Al content and solidification path.
- Inclusion phases: single-type Al₂O₃, AlN, MnS, TiN; composite inclusions Al₂O₃-MnS, AlN-MnS, TiN-MnS, Al₂O₃-AlN-MnS.

## Processing Route and Variables
- Smelting: laboratory-scale induction melting at 1873 K.
- Deoxidation/alloying sequence: ferromanganese added first, then Al as deoxidizer; holding 60 min after Al addition.
- Cooling: furnace cooling to room temperature (slow cooling, near-equilibrium solidification).
- Sample preparation: non-aqueous electrolytic extraction followed by metallographic polishing; inclusion analysis via field-emission scanning electron microscopy (FE-SEM) with energy-dispersive X‑ray spectroscopy (EDS) and automatic inclusion analysis.
- Key process variable: Al content (1, 3, 5 wt%) — the only deliberate compositional change.

## Microstructure and Phase Evolution
- **Solidification paths (FactSage 8.2 vertical sections at 0.4 wt% C):**  
  1# (1Al): L → L+FCC → FCC. Liquidus ≈ 1702 K, solidus ≈ 1588 K.  
  2# (3Al) and 3# (5Al): L → L+BCC → L+BCC+FCC; primary BCC solidification expands. Liquidus increases (1796 K for 3#), solidus decreases (1532 K for 3#). Higher Al narrows austenite region, expands ferrite region.
- **Inclusion types and number percentages (from FE-SEM/EDS):**  
  - 1#: dominant Al₂O₃; also AlN, MnS, TiN; composites Al₂O₃-MnS, AlN-MnS, TiN-MnS, Al₂O₃-AlN-MnS.  
  - 2#: AlN increases; single MnS peaks; Al₂O₃ decreases; Al₂O₃-AlN-MnS present.  
  - 3#: AlN becomes predominant; Al₂O₃ reduced; single MnS lower; Al₂O₃-AlN-MnS absent; AlN-MnS and TiN-MnS decrease.
- **Size distribution (automatic inclusion analysis):**  
  1#: 1–5 μm 58.4%, 5–10 μm 32.3%, >10 μm 9.4%.  
  2#: 53.6%, 34.6%, 11.8%.  
  3#: 48.2%, 46.9%, 4.9%.  
  Trend: with increasing Al, fine fraction (1–5 μm) decreases, medium (5–10 μm) increases; largest fraction peaks at 2# then drops.
- **Morphologies (FE-SEM):**  
  Al₂O₃: elongated, >8 μm; AlN: elongated (~23 μm) or polyhedral; MnS: octahedral (~10 μm); TiN: angular or boot-shaped, up to >30 μm. Composite inclusions show core–shell or multi-layer sequences (e.g., Al₂O₃ core – MnS shell; Al₂O₃ core – AlN middle – MnS outer).

## Processing-Structure-Property Chain
- **Processing → Solidification path:** Al content directly controls liquidus/solidus temperatures and BCC/FCC stability, shifting solidification from fully austenitic (1#) to ferritic–austenitic (2#, 3#).
- **Solidification path → Inclusion precipitation sequence:**  
  - AlN precipitation start solid fraction (*z*ₛ): 1# 0.46, 2# 0.22, 3# 0.51 (non‑monotonic due to altered segregation and N solubility).  
  - TiN precipitation: 1# *z*ₛ = 0.89, 2# 0.93, 3# 0.94 — progressively delayed.  
  - MnS precipitation: 1# *z*ₛ = 0.77, 2# 0.81, 3# 0.85 — progressively delayed.
- **Precipitation sequence → Inclusion population and size:** Early-formed Al₂O₃ and AlN provide heterogeneous nucleation sites for later MnS; the shift in precipitation windows with Al content changes the number percentages of single vs. composite inclusions, and shifts size distributions toward larger inclusions (peak at 5–10 μm in 3#).
- **Microstructural outcome → potential property implications:** Larger, more numerous composite inclusions and shift from fine Al₂O₃ to coarser AlN-dominated inclusion populations may affect steel cleanliness, fatigue initiation, and toughness, though direct mechanical property data are not reported.

## Mechanistic Interpretation
- **Effect of Al on solidification:** Al is a strong BCC stabilizer; higher Al content raises liquidus, lowers solidus, widens primary BCC field, and retards austenite formation. This delays the precipitation of TiN and MnS to higher solid fractions and alters segregation behavior.
- **AlN precipitation anomaly:** The non‑monotonic change in AlN precipitation start fraction (0.46→0.22→0.51) arises because increasing Al from 1% to 3% increases activity of Al, advancing AlN formation, but at 5% Al the strong BCC stabilization and reduced N solubility (segregation ratio of N vs. Al) delay AlN saturation.
- **Heterogeneous nucleation (2D mismatch, Bramfitt model):**  
  - Al₂O₃/MnS: 8.41 %  
  - AlN/MnS: 8.94 %  
  - TiN/MnS: 11.65 %  
  - Al₂O₃/AlN: 11.66 %  
  All values lie in the “moderately effective” range (6–12 %), explaining the experimental observation of core–shell and multi-layer composite inclusions: Al₂O₃, AlN, and TiN serve as effective substrates for MnS precipitation; Al₂O₃ also nucleates AlN.
- **Overall inclusion evolution (schematic Fig. 12):** During smelting, Al₂O₃ forms first. During solidification, AlN, MnS, and TiN precipitate sequentially; hetero‑epitaxy produces complex composites. Higher Al suppresses Al₂O₃ amount, promotes AlN, and delays TiN/MnS, altering the final inclusion population and size distribution.

## Key Quantitative Findings
- Liquidus temperatures: 1# 1702 K, 2# 1763 K, 3# 1796 K (FactSage, 0.4C).
- Solidus temperatures: 1# 1588 K, 2# 1560 K, 3# 1532 K.
- AlN precipitation starts at solid fraction *z*ₛ: 0.46 (1#), 0.22 (2#), 0.51 (3#).
- TiN precipitation *z*ₛ: 0.89 (1#), 0.93 (2#), 0.94 (3#).
- MnS precipitation *z*ₛ: 0.77 (1#), 0.81 (2#), 0.85 (3#).
- Inclusion size distribution (number %):  
  1#: 1–5 μm 58.4%, 5–10 μm 32.3%, >10 μm 9.4%  
  2#: 53.6%, 34.6%, 11.8%  
  3#: 48.2%, 46.9%, 4.9%
- 2D mismatch values: Al₂O₃/MnS 8.41%, AlN/MnS 8.94%, TiN/MnS 11.65%, Al₂O₃/AlN 11.66% — all within moderate‑effectiveness zone (6–12%).
