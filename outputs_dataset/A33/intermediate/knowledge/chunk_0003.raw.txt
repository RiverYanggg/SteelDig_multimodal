    "summary": "This chunk presents detailed mechanical property comparisons and deformation/cracking mechanisms for the duplex low-density steel. It reports that recrystallized austenite grain size grows from 5.13 μm to 9.65 μm with increasing annealing temperature/time (DP1000-1 to DP1050-1), and that granular α ferrite retards grain growth but decreases in number at higher temperature. Annealing at 950 °C gives coarsest δ and α grains which retransform to austenite upon further heating. DP950-1 has highest UTS but lowest TE; extending time to 5 min reduces YS/UTS by ~90 MPa without ductility improvement. The poor ductility of DP950-1/DP950-5 is caused by incompatible straining at ferrite/unrecrystallized austenite interfaces. Raising temperature to 1000 °C fully recrystallizes austenite, reduces ferrite fraction, and harmonizes FCC/BCC deformation, greatly enhancing both YS and TE. Further increase to 1050 °C impairs YS/UTS due to grain growth and dissolution of Cr7C3 and intergranular κ-carbides. DP1000-5 and DP1050-1 achieve SUTS×TE products above 8 GPa·g⁻¹·cm³·%, outperforming Al alloys, Ti64, and other Fe-Mn-Al-C steels. Transverse tensile testing (DP1000-5-TD) shows similar YS/UTS but lower TE; orientation of δ ferrite bands causes δ/γ interfaces to be perpendicular to tensile direction, promoting crack initiation and poor ductility. In DP1000-5, cracks nucleate at α/γ interfaces, propagate along ND, and are deflected/blunted by δ ferrite bands, improving ductility. The overall design uses granular α to restrict grain growth and δ bands to deflect and blunt cracks, with nanosized κ-carbide precipitation from Cr7C3 and κ-carbide dissolution at 1000 °C.
