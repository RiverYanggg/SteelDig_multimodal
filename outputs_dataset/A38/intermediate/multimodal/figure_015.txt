{"image_type": "microscopy_sem", "description": "This figure presents scanning electron microscopy (SEM) images of tensile fracture surfaces from selective laser melted (SLM) Fe–30Mn–9Al–C–3Ni low-density steel specimens processed at varying laser powers. Subfigures (a) through (e) correspond to samples P-70, P-90, P-110, P-130, and P-150 (70 W to 150 W), respectively. At low power (P-70), the fracture surface exhibits severe lack-of-fusion defects, spherical unmelted powder particles, and internal cracks, indicating poor consolidation and ductile-brittle mixed fracture. As power increases to 90 W (P-90) and 110 W (P-110), the morphology transitions to dominant ductile dimple fracture with fine equiaxed dimples visible in the high-magnification inset of (c), signifying superior toughness and energy absorption. However, at higher powers (P-130 and P-150), the surfaces reveal mixed fracture modes combining dimples with transgranular cleavage facets, spheroidization phenomena, and stress-induced cracks. This systematic evolution demonstrates that insufficient energy density causes incomplete melting while excessive power induces thermal stress and solidification defects, with P-110 representing the optimal processing window for mechanical performance.", "confidence": "high"}
