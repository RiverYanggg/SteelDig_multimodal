Subfigures (a) and (b) display lattice strains of individual crystallographic planes—five FCC (austenite) reflections (111, 200, 220, 311, 222) and four BCC (ferrite) reflections (110, 200, 211, 220)—as functions of applied engineering strain, revealing elastic anisotropy and distinct yielding behaviors between phases. Subfigures (c) and (d) show weighted average lattice strains for FCC and BCC phases, with arrows indicating deviation from linearity marking the onset of plastic deformation. In RD samples, austenite yields at 1.8% strain while ferrite yields at 2.3%; in TD samples, these values are 2.1% and 2.9%, respectively. The earlier yielding of austenite demonstrates it is the softer phase, and the directional differences in yielding strains confirm anisotropic deformation behavior related to the banded microstructure of δ-ferrite and ultrafine-grained γ+α aggregates.", "confidence": "high"}
