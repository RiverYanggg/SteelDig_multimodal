Four conditions are compared: solution-treated (SS) and aged (30 h at 550°C) states of both Ti/V-free steel (M1) and Ti/V-modified steel (M2). Subfigure (a) shows that M1-30 achieves yield strength (YS) of 860 MPa and ultimate tensile strength (UTS) of 1004 MPa with 40% elongation, significantly higher than M1-SS (~430 MPa YS), but exhibits reduced work-hardening capacity. M2-30 displays superior strength (YS 884 MPa, UTS 1096 MPa) while maintaining work-hardening comparable to M2-SS (~660 MPa YS). Subfigure (b) reveals that M2-30 maintains high strain hardening rates similar to its solution-treated state, whereas M1-30 shows rapid work-hardening decay. These curves demonstrate that the heterogeneous precipitation of κ-carbides at (Ti,V)C edges in M2-30 avoids glide plane softening observed in M1-30 with uniformly distributed κ-carbides, thereby achieving the synergy of high strength and sustained work-hardening capacity.", "confidence": "high"}
