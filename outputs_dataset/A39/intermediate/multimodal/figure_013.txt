Panel (a) shows the uniformly deformed region of M1-30 (Ti/V-free steel aged at 550°C for 30 h), revealing planar slip bands (indicated by yellow dashed lines) with extensive dislocation networks and relatively wide slip-band spacing. Panel (b) displays the deformed microstructure of M2-30 (Ti/V-modified steel with 0.62 wt% V and 0.09 wt% Ti, aged under identical conditions), where the slip-band spacing is visibly finer and more densely packed than in M1-30, indicating more severe plastic deformation. Panel (c) provides a higher-magnification view (30 nm scale bar) of the blue-boxed region in (b), clearly resolving nano-sized (Ti, V)C carbides (labeled as \"(Ti, V) Carbides\") interacting with dislocations. The images collectively demonstrate that while both steels exhibit planar dislocation slip deformation modes consistent with their high stacking fault energies (~70-78 mJ/m²), the addition of (Ti, V)C carbides in M2-30 hinders dislocation motion and creates regions of higher dislocation density around the carbides. This microstructural difference explains the maintained work-hardening rate in M2-30 compared to M1-30, where homogeneous κ-carbide precipitation causes glide plane softening and reduced work-hardening capacity.", "confidence": "high"}
