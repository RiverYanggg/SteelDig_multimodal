A sharp maximum in work hardening rate is observed for alloys containing 26–30% Al. Dislocation structure analysis reveals a transition from single bowed dislocations to paired superdislocations with straight screw segments and sessile reaction products as Al content increases. The peak work hardening is attributed to strong Peierls forces and superdislocation dipole formation rather than directly to elastic anisotropy.",
