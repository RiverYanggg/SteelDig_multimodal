# Material System
The alloy investigated is an austenitic Fe–30Mn–6.5Al–0.3C (wt.%) low‑density steel. It crystallises as a single face‑centred cubic (fcc) phase and is free of ordered κ‑carbides, as confirmed by selected‑area electron diffraction along the [101] zone axis, which shows fundamental fcc reflections without extra spots at ⟨200⟩ or ⟨220⟩ positions. Hydrogen charging introduces 101 mass ppm H into solid solution; thermal desorption analysis reveals a single desorption peak centred at ≈700 K, signifying predominately lattice‑dissolved hydrogen with negligible trapping at grain boundaries or precipitates. The uncharged reference condition contains less than 5 mass ppm H.

# Processing Route and Variables
- **Material preparation:** Fully austenitic Fe–30Mn–6.5Al–0.3C (wt.%) steel, single‑phase fcc and κ‑carbide‑free.
- **Hydrogen charging:** Gaseous charging at 100 MPa H₂ and 543 K for 200 h → 101 mass ppm total hydrogen; confirmed by thermal desorption analysis (heating rate 100 K h⁻¹, thermal‑conductivity detector).
- **Mechanical testing:** Room‑temperature (295 K) uniaxial tension at an initial strain rate of 5.0 × 10⁻⁵ s⁻¹ to various strain levels; the end of uniform elongation corresponds to ε ≈ 0.4.
- **Microstructural characterisation:** Electron channelling contrast imaging (ECCI), electron backscatter diffraction (EBSD), bright‑field scanning transmission electron microscopy (BF‑STEM) under two‑beam conditions with {111}‑type diffraction vectors, and correlative misorientation profiling.
- **Texture and orientation dependence:** Deformation structures are classified according to grain orientation relative to the tensile axis (TA): ⟨111⟩ // TA, ⟨001⟩ // TA, and the [112]–[101] fibre region.

# Microstructure and Phase Evolution
## Classification of dislocation structures before and after hydrogen charging (ε = 0.4, room temperature)
**Type 1a cell blocks:** Elongated cell blocks (CBs) formed under single or twofold slip oriented along the [112]–[101] fibre. Primary (GNB1) and secondary (GNB2) geometrically necessary boundary (GNB) systems run parallel to {111} slip‑plane traces; incidental dislocation boundaries (IDBs) appear between GNBs.  
**Type 1b cell blocks:** Rhombic cell blocks generated by two intersecting GNB systems (GNB1 and GNB2/GNB2s).  
**Type 2 dislocation cells (near ⟨001⟩ // TA):** Equiaxed dislocation cells (DCs) produced by octahedral slip on multiple systems. In the uncharged state the average cell size D̄DC ≈ 220 ± 10 nm; hydrogen charging refines this to D̄DC ^H ≈ 192 ± 10 nm. Additionally, macroscopic deformation bands (DBs, 10–30 μm thick) subdivide ⟨001⟩‑oriented grains in the charged condition.  
**Type 3 mixed structures (near ⟨111⟩ // TA):** Complex arrangements containing DCs, dense dislocation walls (DDWs), deformation twins (DTs), and microbands (MBs). In the uncharged state the average DC size is D̄DC ≈ 236 ± 10 nm; hydrogen charging reduces it to D̄DC ^H ≈ 212 ± 10 nm, while the average cell‑boundary misorientation remains essentially unchanged (θ̄DC ≈ 0.87° → θ̄DC ^H ≈ 0.90°). Deformation bands of 0.5–1.0 μm thickness and plate‑type microbands (150–200 nm thick) become more prominent upon hydrogen charging.

## Hydrogen‑induced modifications
- **Hydrogen‑enhanced deformation banding (HEDB):** Macroscopic kink bands (10–30 μm thick) appear in ⟨001⟩‑oriented grains; sub‑micron DBs (0.5–1.0 μm) form in ⟨111⟩‑oriented grains. BF‑STEM reveals that DB–DDW intersections exhibit larger shear offsets (≈75–100 nm) in charged samples vs. negligible offsets in uncharged samples.
- **Hydrogen‑enhanced microbanding (HEMB):** Localised microbands nucleate along GNB segments. MBs are lenticular, 150–200 nm thick and 1.5–4 μm long, and exhibit misorientation angles >2°. The critical threshold is a GNB misorientation: <2° = dense dislocation walls; >2° = microbands. In Type 1a, hydrogen increases GNB1 misorientation from 1.62° to 2.70° and IDB misorientation from 1.14° to 1.38°. In Type 1b, GNB1 increases from 2.64° to 3.65° and GNB2 from 2.67° to 3.71°.
- **Dislocation cells:** Hydrogen systematically refines cell size in Type 2 (220 → 192 nm) and Type 3 (236 → 212 nm) structures, while cell‑boundary misorientation in Type 3 is nearly constant (0.87° → 0.90°).

# Processing‑Structure‑Property Chain
**Processing:** Gaseous hydrogen charging at 100 MPa H₂, 543 K for 200 h → 101 mass ppm H uniformly dissolved in the fcc lattice (Figure 1).  
**Deformation:** Room‑temperature tension at 5.0 × 10⁻⁵ s⁻¹ to ε = 0.4.  
**Structure:** Hydrogen does not alter the material’s stacking fault energy sufficiently to induce deformation twinning; the four‑stage strain‑hardening sequence is preserved. HEDB and HEMB appear as additional strain‑accommodation features: macroscopic DBs in ⟨001⟩ grains, sub‑micron DBs in ⟨111⟩ grains, and GNBs decorated by microbands with enhanced misorientations. Dislocation cells become finer, and DDW/DC structures are sheared by DBs with measurable offsets (≈75–100 nm).  
**Property:** The hydrogen‑charged specimen (101 mass ppm H) exhibits yield strength ≈ 302 MPa (uncharged ≈ 275 MPa, + ~10%), ultimate tensile strength ≈ 595 MPa (uncharged ≈ 580 MPa), and uniform elongation increases from 0.43 to 0.46. The index of hydrogen embrittlement is IHE = −0.045 (ε_f^H / ε_f > 1), qualifying the steel as highly resistant, with ductile fracture showing refined dimples (1.5–3.0 μm vs. 2–8 μm uncharged).

# Mechanistic Interpretation
**Origin of HEDB and HEMB:** Dissolved hydrogen reduces the critical stress for dislocation motion on secondary slip systems and promotes double‑cross‑slip of screw dislocations. In ⟨001⟩ grains, far‑from‑primary slip, this activates macroscopic DBs; in ⟨111⟩ grains, slip is concentrated on fewer systems, and hydrogen promotes sub‑micron DBs together with DDW formation.  
**GNB‑assisted microband nucleation:** Hydrogen enhances the storage of geometrically necessary dislocations (GNDs) at pre‑existing GNBs, increasing their misorientation. When the GNB misorientation exceeds ~2°, the boundary splits into a pair of dislocation sheets (microband), which accommodates localised shear.  
**DB‑assisted microband nucleation:** Inside DBs, high densities of screw and mixed dislocations (≈1–3 × 10¹⁴ m⁻²) undergo cross‑slip and double‑cross‑slip, shearing primary DDW/DC structures. Local dislocation reactions, e.g. Lomer‑Cottrell lock formation, produce microbands that carry additional plastic shear.  
**Role in hydrogen embrittlement resistance:** HEMB creates sub‑micron strain gradients that store GNDs, enabling plastic relaxation of stress concentrations at DB intersections and grain boundaries. This relaxation delays or prevents damage nucleation, preserving ductile microvoid‑coalescence fracture with refined dimples. The lack of brittle intergranular or quasi‑cleavage facets confirms that hydrogen‑induced plasticity (HEDB/HEMB) mitigates, rather than promotes, embrittlement in this alloy.

# Key Quantitative Findings
- **Hydrogen content:** Charged: 101 mass ppm; uncharged: <5 mass ppm (TDA, single peak at ≈700 K).
- **Tensile properties** (295 K, 5.0 × 10⁻⁵ s⁻¹):
  - Yield strength: ≈275 MPa (uncharged) → ≈302 MPa (charged, +~10%).
  - UTS: ≈580 MPa → ≈595 MPa.
  - Uniform elongation: 0.43 → 0.46 (+~8%).
  - Hydrogen embrittlement index: IHE = −0.045 (< 0, extremely high resistance).
- **GNB misorientation increases (Type 1a):** GNB1: 1.62° → 2.70°; IDB: 1.14° → 1.38°.
- **GNB misorientation increases (Type 1b):** GNB1: 2.64° → 3.65°; GNB2: 2.67° → 3.71°.
- **Microband geometry:** Thickness 150–200 nm, length 1.5–4 μm, formed along {111} slip‑plane traces.
- **Deformation band geometry:** Macroscopic DBs 10–30 μm (⟨001⟩ grains); sub‑micron DBs 0.5–1.0 μm (⟨111⟩ grains); shear offsets at DB–DDW intersections ≈75–100 nm (charged) vs. negligible (uncharged).
- **Dislocation‑cell refinement:** Type 2: 220 ± 10 nm → 192 ± 10 nm; Type 3: 236 ± 10 nm → 212 ± 10 nm.
- **Fracture dimple size:** Uncharged 2–8 μm → charged 1.5–3.0 μm (fully ductile in both conditions).
- **Dislocation density in DBs:** ≈1–3 × 10¹⁴ m⁻², predominantly screw and mixed character.
