# Material System
- **Base Alloy (0Al)**: Fe-0.32C-1.98Si-1.02Mn-1.00Cr-3.41Ni-0.56Mo-0.09V-0.03Al (wt%), a low-alloy ultrahigh-strength steel serving as the reference martensitic steel without intentional Al addition.
- **Modified Alloy (1.3Al)**: Fe-0.31C-2.01Si-1.03Mn-1.00Cr-5.06Ni-0.60Mo-0.11V-1.30Al (wt%), designed for dual precipitation of B2-NiAl nanoclusters and ε-carbides.
- **Key Precipitates**: B2-NiAl clusters (number density ≈4.88×10²⁴ m⁻³, equivalent radius ≈0.57 nm, lattice misfit <3% with α-Fe, fully coherent) co-existing with submicron ε-carbides (hcp, thickness ≈2.2 nm, length 100–500 nm).
- **Matrix**: Tempered lath martensite containing thin films of retained austenite (0Al: 1.0 vol%; 1.3Al: 1.8 vol%) and high dislocation density (0Al: ≈2.01×10¹⁵ m⁻²; 1.3Al: ≈2.20×10¹⁵ m⁻²).

# Processing Route and Variables
- **Processing Route**: Vacuum melting → forging → solution treatment at 970°C for 1 h → water quench (WQ) → tempering at 180°C for 2 h → water quench.
- **Compositional Variables**: Al content increased from 0.03 wt% (0Al) to 1.30 wt% (1.3Al); Ni correspondingly increased from 3.41 wt% to 5.06 wt% to maintain NiAl stoichiometry considerations.
- **Sample Designations**: 0Al,WQ; 0Al,2h (tempered); 1.3Al,WQ; 1.3Al,2h (tempered).
- **Mechanical Test Conditions**: Room-temperature tensile (strain rate 0.001 s⁻¹, gauge length 25 mm, diameter 5 mm); Charpy U-notch impact (KU2, 10×10×55 mm³); plane-strain fracture toughness KQ (3-point bending, 25 mm thickness, 50 mm width, 3 mm fatigue pre-crack, loading rate 38 kN/min, per ASTM E399).

# Microstructure and Phase Evolution
- **Martensitic Hierarchy**: Both 0Al and 1.3Al steels exhibit statistically comparable martensitic unit sizes—prior austenite grain size ≈23–26 μm, packet size ≈3.4–3.5 μm, block width ≈0.8–0.9 μm, and lath width ≈0.23–0.24 μm; microstructural refinement is not the primary origin of toughness differences.
- **Phase Constitution**: Tempered martensite (bcc) + retained austenite (fcc) films at lath boundaries (Kurdjumov-Sachs orientation relationship); retained austenite fraction increases from 1.0% (0Al) to 1.8% (1.3Al).
- **Precipitate Evolution in 1.3Al,2h**: High-density NiAl clusters (radius ≈0.57 nm, number density ≈4.88×10²⁴ m⁻³) uniformly distributed in the matrix; ε-carbides (thickness ≈2.2 nm, spacing ~10 nm) independently nucleated without competitive partitioning; no carbide coarsening or transformation to cementite at this temper.
- **Solute Partitioning & Heterogeneous Nucleation**: APT reveals co-segregation of Si (enriching from ~4.2 at% to ~11.2 at%), Cr, and Mo at non-dislocation intragranular defects, providing heterogeneous nucleation sites for NiAl clusters; Si enhances nucleation of both ε-carbides and NiAl clusters via solute-drag or defect-stabilization mechanisms.

# Processing-Structure-Property Chain
- **Composition → Nano-precipitation**: Adding 1.30 wt% Al and raising Ni to 5.06 wt% enables formation of extremely high-density coherent NiAl clusters upon low-temperature tempering (180°C/2h), while Si promotes heterogeneous nucleation of both NiAl clusters and ε-carbides.
- **Precipitate State → Dislocation Behavior**: NiAl clusters, fully coherent with the martensite matrix and with <3% misfit, homogenize dislocation distribution and suppress planar slip and pile-ups; 1.3Al,2h exhibits a more uniform strain distribution (average KAM 1.31°) compared to 0Al,2h (average KAM 1.16° with localized high-strain zones).
- **Dislocation Configurations → Mechanical Response**: Dislocation homogenization delays micro-void nucleation, reduces local stress concentration, and promotes uniform plastic deformation; 1.3Al,2h achieves ≈1876 MPa UTS and ≈13.7% total elongation, while 0Al,2h reaches ≈1925 MPa UTS and ≈13.5% elongation.
- **Toughness Enhancement**: Charpy impact energy KU2 increases from 65 ± 3 J (0Al,2h) to 80 ± 3 J (1.3Al,2h)—a 23% relative gain—while fracture toughness KQ rises from 97.6 MPa·m¹/² to 108.0 MPa·m¹/². Crack initiation energy improved by 37.8% (45.0 J → 62.0 J) and crack propagation energy improved by 20.7% (17.4 J → 21.0 J) in the 1.3Al steel.
- **Fracture Mode Transition**: 0Al,2h shows predominantly quasi-cleavage fracture with limited ductile dimples; 1.3Al,2h exhibits extensive dimple growth, tearing ridges, and higher surface roughness, indicating a shift toward micro-void coalescence-dominated ductile fracture.
- **Strength–Toughness Trade-off Mitigated**: At ~1.9 GPa UTS level, the 1.3Al steel simultaneously improves elongation, impact energy, and fracture toughness compared to the 0Al steel, demonstrating that high-density coherent NiAl clusters decouple strength from toughness.

# Mechanistic Interpretation
- **Independent Nucleation Mechanism**: ε-carbides and NiAl clusters nucleate independently with no competitive elemental partitioning; ε-carbides are C-rich (~8.91 at% C in core), NiAl clusters are enriched in Ni+Al (~13 at% summed), and the surrounding matrix retains low carbon (~1.0 at%), eliminating the traditional competition between carbide-forming and intermetallic-forming solutes.
- **Dislocation–Precipitate Interaction**: Fully coherent NiAl clusters with <3% lattice misfit provide weak but extremely dense obstacles that operate via a dislocation–bypass or looping mechanism under load, promoting distributed dislocation multiplication and suppressing localized planar slip bands; this homogenization reduces the driving force for premature crack initiation at grain boundaries or packet boundaries.
- **Multi-Scale Toughening Mechanisms**: (i) Uniform dislocation distribution increases the energy barrier for crack initiation (higher Ei); (ii) extensive micro-void nucleation at precipitates leads to large dimple growth absorbing plastic work; (iii) secondary cracks 5–10 μm and micro-voids coalescence 8–18 μm beneath the main fracture surface create crack deflection paths that dissipate energy; (iv) tortuous crack propagation and crack branching raise total propagation energy Ep.
- **Strain Delocalization**: EBSD-KAM maps confirm that 1.3Al steel exhibits a more homogeneous strain distribution near fracture surfaces, facilitating stress transfer from the crack tip to the interior matrix and enabling coordinated deformation, whereas 0Al steel concentrates strain near the crack.

# Key Quantitative Findings
- **Composition and Processing**: 0Al (0.03Al-3.41Ni) vs 1.3Al (1.30Al-5.06Ni); 970°C/1h WQ + 180°C/2h temper.
- **Precipitate Parameters (1.3Al,2h)**: NiAl cluster equivalent radius ≈0.57 nm, number density ≈4.88×10²⁴ m⁻³; ε-carbide thickness ≈2.2 nm, C core content ≈8.91 at%; Si enrichment at defects from ≈4.2 to ≈11.2 at%.
- **Dislocation Density**: 0Al ≈2.01×10¹⁵ m⁻², 1.3Al ≈2.20×10¹⁵ m⁻² (tempered state).
- **Tensile Properties (tempered)**: 0Al: UTS ≈1925 MPa, elongation 13.5%; 1.3Al: UTS ≈1876 MPa, elongation 13.7%.
- **Impact Toughness (KU2, tempered)**: 0Al: 65 ± 3 J (Ei=45.0 J, Ep=17.4 J); 1.3Al: 80 ± 3 J (Ei=62.0 J, Ep=21.0 J).
- **Fracture Toughness (tempered)**: 0Al: KQ ≈97.6 MPa·m¹/²; 1.3Al: KQ ≈108.0 MPa·m¹/².
- **Microstructural Constancy**: Prior austenite grain, packet, block, and lath dimensions statistically identical between the two steels (figure_005 support).
