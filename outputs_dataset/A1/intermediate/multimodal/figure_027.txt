Panel (a) shows Fe-30Mn-1.2C-xAl (high-carbon austenitic/TWIP steel), while panel (b) displays Fe-30Mn-0.4C-xAl (low-carbon duplex steel). All steels were solutionized at 1100°C for 2 hours, with solid curves representing water-quenched samples and dotted curves representing samples subsequently aged at 550°C for 24 hours. Each subplot plots ultimate tensile strength (UTS, magenta squares), yield strength (YS, blue diamonds), total elongation (TE, black triangles), and δ (green/yellow inverted triangles) against Al content (0–12 wt%). For the 1.2C series, UTS initially decreases then increases sharply above 8% Al, with significant age-hardening effects only appearing when Al exceeds 8% due to κ-carbide precipitation. The 0.4C series shows minimum strength at 2% Al (fully austenitic, maximum ductility), followed by increasing strength but decreasing elongation as δ-ferrite forms at higher Al contents.
