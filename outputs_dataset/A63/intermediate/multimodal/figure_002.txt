Subfigure (b) reveals the Zr0.01 steel predominantly containing coarse FCC grains with a small fraction (~6%) of fine FCC grains indicated by dashed cyan lines, showing an average grain size reduction to ~7 μm. The progressive development of heterogeneous grain structure with increasing Zr content from 0 to 0.50 wt% demonstrates the transition from homogeneous coarse grains to a dual-phase morphology, attributed to Zr solute drag and Zener pinning effects by ZrC nanoparticles.
