{"image_type": "microscopy_optical", "description": "Figure 2 presents optical micrographs of Fe-Mn-Al-C alloys demonstrating the effect of manganese content and heat treatment on microstructural evolution. The set comprises four micrographs: images I and II show alloys with 25% Mn and 20% Mn respectively (both containing 10% Al and 1% C), with subfigures (a) representing the quenched condition from 1150°C (×150) and (b) showing the aged condition at 600°C for 50 hours (×500). In the quenched state, the 25% Mn alloy exhibits single-phase austenite (γ), whereas the 20% Mn alloy displays a duplex structure of austenite and ferrite (α). After aging, the 25% Mn alloy reveals dispersed phase precipitation at grain boundaries with enhanced etchability, while the 20% Mn alloy shows retained austenite with acicular morphology surrounding ferrite particles, alongside carbide precipitation at austenite grain boundaries. These microstructural changes correlate with a sharp hardness increase from HRB 86-88 to HRC 35-37, confirming that aging induces γ-phase decomposition and carbide formation, thereby enabling precipitation hardening in these alloys.", "confidence": "high"}
