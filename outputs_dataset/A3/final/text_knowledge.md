# Material System
Three austenitic Fe-Mn-Al-C-(Ni) low-density steels: (i) **28Mn0Ni**: Fe–28Mn–9Al–0.9C (Ni-free), (ii) **28Mn5Ni**: Fe–28Mn–9Al–0.9C–5Ni, (iii) **15Mn5Ni**: Fe–15Mn–9Al–0.9C–5Ni (lower Mn, with Ni). The matrix phase is austenite (γ, FCC); secondary phases include κ-carbide and B2 (Fe(Ni)Al) ordered intermetallic. In some conditions D03 ((Fe,Ni)3Al) is expected.

# Processing Route and Variables
All alloys were produced by vacuum induction melting, homogenized at 1423 K for 2.5 h, then hot rolled at 1323 K (HR condition). Room-temperature cold rolling to 50% reduction in thickness gave the HR+CR condition. Heat treatment of the cold-rolled material was performed in the range 973–1173 K for 1 h followed by water quenching.

# Microstructure and Phase Evolution
**Prior to cold rolling** the hot-rolled 15Mn5Ni contained a high initial volume fraction of B2 phase (~0.4 based on later measurements), while 28Mn5Ni contained about ~0.11 B2 and 28Mn0Ni was essentially single-phase austenite.

**After cold rolling (HR+CR)**:
- *28Mn0Ni*: deformed austenite grains with parallel planar slip bands; EBSD phase map confirms single FCC phase.
- *28Mn5Ni*: deformed austenite with sparse B2 precipitates visible as blue spots in phase maps.
- *15Mn5Ni*: extensive B2 stringers aligned along the rolling direction embedded in the FCC matrix; the brittle B2 stringers undergo shearing and fragmentation during cold rolling (yellow arrows in B2 IPF inset highlight this behavior).

**After cold rolling + heat treatment at 973 K (1 h)**:
XRD confirms that in both 28Mn0Ni and 15Mn5Ni, κ-carbide and B2 peaks appear alongside γ-austenite after 973 K annealing; the B2 peaks are more intense in 15Mn5Ni than in 28Mn0Ni.
TEM (28Mn0Ni): fine uniformly distributed intragranular κ-carbides in recrystallized austenite regions; deformed austenite exhibits inhomogeneous, coarser κ-carbides of 10–60 nm.
TEM (28Mn5Ni & 15Mn5Ni): fine intragranular B2 particles (5–20 nm) plus coarser intergranular B2 (50–200 nm) coexist with κ‑carbides. Deformed regions show higher density/coarser carbides, indicating strain-enhanced nucleation.

**After cold rolling + heat treatment at 1023 K (1 h)**:
- *28Mn0Ni*: fully recrystallized equiaxed austenite grains, minimal B2, low KAM (strain-free).
- *28Mn5Ni*: partially recrystallized elongated grains with limited B2 at grain boundaries and moderate strain localization.
- *15Mn5Ni*: heavily banded microstructure with extensive B2 decoration along boundaries; high local strain concentration (green/red KAM).

**After cold rolling + heat treatment at 1173 K (1 h)**:
- *28Mn0Ni*: single-phase austenite, no secondary precipitates; pronounced grain coarsening (recrystallized grain size up to ~40 μm).
- *28Mn5Ni*: both intragranular (yellow dashed arrows) and intergranular (white arrows) B2 particles persist; finer grain structure due to B2 pinning.
- *15Mn5Ni*: B2 precipitates remain at grain boundaries and within grains; finer B2 particles dissolve preferentially, while B2 stringers are resistant.

**Quantitative trends from EBSD analysis (heat treatment temperature sweep)** (Figure 5):
(a) Recrystallization fraction of austenite: 28Mn0Ni reaches ~0.9 at 1023 K; both Ni-containing alloys (28Mn5Ni, 15Mn5Ni) remain ~0.4–0.5 even at 1173 K → **Ni delays recrystallization**.
(b) Recrystallized austenite grain size: 28Mn0Ni ~40 μm at 1173 K; 28Mn5Ni and 15Mn5Ni <5 μm at 1173 K → **B2 particles suppress grain growth**.
(c) Twin fraction: 28Mn0Ni ~0.55 at 1173 K; 28Mn5Ni ~0.35; 15Mn5Ni ~0.2 → lower twin fractions in B2‑containing alloys.
(d) B2 volume fraction: 15Mn5Ni ~0.4 at 973 K decreasing with temperature; 28Mn5Ni ~0.11 and follows similar dissolution trend.

# Processing-Structure-Property Chain
**Hardness evolution** (Vickers):
- All three alloys show substantial work hardening: after 50% cold rolling, hardness converges to ~430–460 VHN, up from ~250 VHN (28Mn0Ni), ~260 VHN (28Mn5Ni), and ~380 VHN (15Mn5Ni) in the hot-rolled state.
- On annealing (873–1273 K): 28Mn0Ni shows steepest decline (~430 → ~190 VHN); 15Mn5Ni maintains highest hardness (~500 → ~300 VHN); 28Mn5Ni intermediate.
  - Interpretation: Ni + low Mn retain hardness at high temperatures due to B2 + κ‑carbide pinning of recrystallization and grain growth.

**Tensile properties** (HR+CR vs. heat treated):
- As cold-rolled (HR+CR):
  - 28Mn0Ni: YS ~1350 MPa (estimated from stress-strain curves), limited elongation.
  - 28Mn5Ni: peak as-rolled YS/UTS and limited ductility.
  - 15Mn5Ni: YS ~1665 MPa, UTS ~1800 MPa, elongation 5–11%; highest strength but low ductility.
- Heat treated at 973 K:
  - 28Mn0Ni: progressive strength decrease, ductility improvement; partially recrystallized.
  - 28Mn5Ni: anomalous peak strength (YS ~1450 MPa, UTS ~1645 MPa) but drastically reduced elongation (~5%) → attributed to concurrent κ‑carbide + B2 precipitation strengthening combined with insufficient recrystallization.
  - 15Mn5Ni: brittle behavior at lower heat treatment temperatures due to high B2 stringer volume fraction and κ‑carbides at grain boundaries (intergranular cracking).
- Heat treated at 1023 K:
  - 15Mn5Ni: negligible ductility (<1%); fracture analysis shows quasi‑cleavage with microcracks along γ/B2 interphase boundaries; phase map confirms crack propagation through precipitate‑rich grain boundaries.
- Heat treated at 1173 K:
  - 28Mn0Ni: fully recrystallized, YS ~504 MPa, elongation ~86%.
- Strain‑hardening exponent (n) from ln(σ)–ln(ε):
  - 28Mn0Ni: n increases from ~0.16 (973 K) to ~0.50 (1173 K).
  - Ni‑containing alloys (28Mn5Ni, 15Mn5Ni): lower n values (~0.30 at 1173 K).
  - Inference: fully recrystallized, precipitate‑free austenite (28Mn0Ni at 1173 K) exhibits superior strain‑hardening capacity.

# Mechanistic Interpretation
**Ni effect on recrystallization and precipitation**: Ni addition promotes B2 phase formation and stabilizes B2 stringers during annealing. B2 particles (intergranular and intragranular) pin grain boundaries and retard recrystallization, maintaining fine grains but sacrificing ductility when B2 fraction is high.

**Mn effect on phase stability**: Lower Mn (15Mn5Ni) increases driving force for B2 formation, yielding a high initial B2 fraction; higher Mn (28Mn) stabilizes austenite, limiting B2 and enhancing recrystallization.

**Strain hardening mechanisms**:
- In fully recrystallized 28Mn0Ni (1173 K), post‑deformation TEM shows planar slip bands with homogeneous spacing (~200±30 nm) that dynamically refine with strain. Dislocation‑twin interactions are active.
  → Dominant hardening modes: planar slip, dynamic slip band refinement, dislocation‑twin interactions.
- In partially recrystallized or precipitate‑containing microstructures (e.g., 28Mn0Ni at 973 K), dislocation cells form by fine cross‑slip in deformed austenite regions.
- In 15Mn5Ni, dislocation‑B2 particle interactions (pinning/bowing) evolve into refined slip bands with increasing strain; precipitate‑dislocation interactions contribute additional hardening but can embrittle interfaces.

**Fracture transition**: 28Mn0Ni and 28Mn5Ni fail by ductile dimple fracture; 15Mn5Ni at 1023 K fails by quasi‑cleavage with crack initiation along γ/B2 interphase boundaries — a direct consequence of high intergranular B2 fraction.
