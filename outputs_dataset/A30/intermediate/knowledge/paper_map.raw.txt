  "abstract_summary": "The study investigates the hot deformation behavior of an Fe-8% Al ferritic lightweight steel using torsion tests at 900–1100 °C and strain rates of 0.1–10 s⁻¹. EBSD microstructural analysis reveals that two dynamic recrystallization mechanisms operate depending on conditions: continuous dynamic recrystallization (CDRX) at lower temperatures and strain rates, and discontinuous dynamic recrystallization (DDRX) at higher temperatures and strain rates. The competition between CDRX and DDRX governs the evolution of grain boundaries, subgrains, and crystallographic texture, with DDRX producing randomly oriented grains beneficial for reducing roping defects.",
