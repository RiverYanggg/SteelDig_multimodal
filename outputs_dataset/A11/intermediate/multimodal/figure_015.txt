The figure reveals that when the Zener-Hollomon parameter (Z) is transiently increased (higher strain rate or lower temperature), the DRX fraction in the second stage initially lags behind constant-condition predictions due to delayed nucleation of new DRX grains (DRXGsII) and the refinement of existing DRX grains from the first stage (DRXGsI).
