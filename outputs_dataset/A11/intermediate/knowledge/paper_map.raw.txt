  "title": "Quantification of multi-stage recrystallization in low-alloy steel under varying deformation conditions using inhomogeneous-dislocation-density 3D cellular automaton",
  "abstract_summary": "This work develops a 3D cellular automaton model incorporating dislocation density gradients and grain-boundary sliding to predict dynamic recrystallization (DRX) microstructure and flow stress of low-alloy steel under varying temperature and strain rate conditions. The influence of transient changes in the Zener-Hollomon parameter ZP on multi-stage DRX kinetics, stress softening, and final grain size is investigated. A modified phenomenological constitutive model accounts for varying 50% DRX time and residual softening from prior stages. Simulations reveal that an increase in ZP refines first-stage DRX grains but delays second-stage nucleation, leading to larger final grain size, while a decrease in ZP produces inhomogeneous dislocation density that accelerates grain growth to sizes similar to constant conditions, accurately matching experimental stress-decrease rates.",
