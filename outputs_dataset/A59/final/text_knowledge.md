# Material System
Low-density δ-ferrite steel with high Al content (>4 wt%) featuring phases including δ-ferrite, martensite, ultra-fine spherical carbides (cementite-type M₃C, M₂₃C₆, M₇C₃), and deformation-induced ferrite (DIF).

Alloy composition (wt%): Fe-0.35C-4.5Al-1.47Mn-1.47Ni-0.26Cr-0.28Mo.

# Processing Route and Variables
The steel was processed via vacuum induction melting, homogenized at 1200°C for 4 h, hot forged to a thickness of 40 mm, and air cooled. For controlled rolling, specimens were reheated to four distinct temperatures: 650°C, 750°C, 850°C, and 950°C, held for 2 h, and subjected to multi-pass hot rolling with a 60% reduction ratio (thickness from 40 mm to 16 mm) followed by air cooling. The resulting sample groups are designated R650, R750, R850, and R950. The Ac₁ critical temperature serves as a reference: below ~750°C, carbides are thermodynamically stable, while above ~850°C, carbides dissolve and austenite transforms to martensite upon cooling.

# Microstructure and Phase Evolution
- **Low-temperature rolling (R650, R750)**: Produces a continuous banded lamellar structure consisting of alternating δ-ferrite lamellae and carbide/ferrite (C/F) composite lamellae. In R650, the volume fraction of C/F composite lamellae is 51.5%, containing ultra-fine spherical carbides with an average diameter of 158.3 ± 76.9 nm and a number density of 1.7 ± 0.2 μm⁻². At R750, the C/F lamellae fraction decreases to 43.3%, and carbides coarsen to 247.3 ± 86.8 nm with a reduced density of 0.6 ± 0.1 μm⁻², exhibiting a bimodal size distribution due to Ostwald ripening.
- **High-temperature rolling (R850, R950)**: Carbides dissolve completely. The microstructure transitions to a duplex lamellar morphology comprising δ-ferrite lamellae and honeycomb martensite lamellae with embedded equiaxed deformation-induced ferrite (DIF) grains. The δ-ferrite fraction increases to 66.8% at R850 then decreases to 41.0% at R950, while the martensite fraction rises significantly.
- **EBSD characteristics**: Low-temperature samples (R650, R750) exhibit higher kernel average misorientation (KAM 0.48–0.55°) indicating higher dislocation density, lower fractions of recrystallized grains (GOS ≤ 2°: 6.9–12.3%), and fewer high-angle grain boundaries (HAGBs 43.5–49.6%). High-temperature samples (R850, R950) show lower KAM (0.3–0.4°), higher recrystallized fractions (19.4–25.8%), and more HAGBs (60.1–75.7%). The crystallographic texture evolves from predominant ∼ ⟨001⟩ in low-temperature samples to ∼ ⟨101⟩ in high-temperature samples.
- **Carbide phases** (TEM/SAED/EDS in R650): Identified as M₃C, M₂₃C₆, and M₇C₃ (M = Mn, Cr). Elemental mapping reveals Mn and Cr enrichment with Al depletion inside the carbides. These carbides precipitate within the ferrite matrix of composite lamellae, originating from martensite decomposition during low-temperature rolling.

# Processing-Structure-Property Chain
- **Impact toughness**: Room-temperature Charpy V-notch impact energy drops drastically with increasing rolling temperature: R650 exhibits 207.5 ± 29.3 J, while R950 yields only 4.6 ± 0.2 J. The load–displacement curves for R650 show a distinct crack propagation stage (Ep = 147.9 J) and crack initiation energy (Ei = 59.6 J), whereas R850/R950 present a single brittle peak with negligible Ei (∼3.9 J) and Ep (∼2.1 J). This toughness degradation is directly linked to the microstructural transition from carbide-reinforced composite lamellae to a hard martensite/DIF duplex structure with high interfacial hardness mismatch.
- **Tensile properties**: As rolling temperature increases, ultimate tensile strength (UTS) rises from 868.7 MPa (R650) to 1254.5 MPa (R950), but total elongation falls from 19.0% to 3.5%. The work-hardening rate curves of low-temperature rolled specimens display a characteristic “hump” attributed to the Orowan mechanism (dislocation–carbide interactions), whereas high-temperature specimens exhibit monotonic decay without appreciable strain hardening.
- **Local hardness**: Nanoindentation measurements reveal two hardness plateaus in R650: δ-ferrite lamellae ∼2.9 GPa and C/F composite lamellae ∼3.6 GPa. In contrast, R850/R950 show three hardness levels: martensite 6.0–6.5 GPa, DIF ∼2.8–3.0 GPa, and δ-ferrite 2.6–2.8 GPa. The severe mismatch (>3 GPa) between martensite and ferrite phases promotes brittle fracture, while the moderate gradient in R650 favors crack blunting.

# Mechanistic Interpretation
- **Orowan strengthening**: Ultra-fine spherical carbides (size < 250 nm) in R650 act as strong obstacles to dislocation motion, contributing to the work-hardening hump and enhanced strain hardening capacity.
- **Crack tip blunting**: When a crack approaches a carbide/ferrite interface in composite lamellae, the nanoscale carbides increase the effective curvature radius of the crack tip, reducing stress concentration and arresting crack propagation.
- **Crack deflection**: Cracks are forced to deflect and propagate horizontally along the ferrite/carbide lamellar interfaces rather than penetrating vertically, as evidenced by elongated crack paths parallel to the lamellae in impact-deformed zones.
- **Microvoid-induced crack propagation**: Microvoids nucleate at carbide/ferrite interfaces, promoting energy dissipation and blunting the main crack. This mechanism further delays catastrophic fracture.
- **High-temperature brittleness**: In R850/R950, the absence of carbides and the presence of hard martensite lamellae lead to a sharp hardness mismatch, concentrating stress at phase boundaries and enabling brittle cleavage with minimal crack deflection. Cracks take shortcuts through DIF grains, resulting in very low impact energies.

# Key Quantitative Findings
- Impact energy: R650 207.5 ± 29.3 J, R950 4.6 ± 0.2 J.
- Crack initiation energy (Ei) for R650: 59.6 J; propagation energy (Ep): 147.9 J. For R850/R950: Ei ∼3.9 J, Ep ∼2.1 J.
- UTS / Elongation: R650 868.7 MPa, 19.0%; R950 1254.5 MPa, 3.5%.
- Carbide size and density: R650 158.3 ± 76.9 nm, 1.7 ± 0.2 μm⁻²; R750 247.3 ± 86.8 nm, 0.6 ± 0.1 μm⁻².
- C/F composite lamellae volume fraction: R650 51.5%, R750 43.3%.
- δ-ferrite volume fraction: R850 66.8%, R950 41.0%.
- Nanoindentation hardness: R650 δ-ferrite ∼2.9 GPa, C/F composite ∼3.6 GPa; R850/R950 martensite 6.0–6.5 GPa, DIF ∼2.8–3.0 GPa, δ-ferrite 2.6–2.8 GPa.
- Recrystallized fraction (GOS ≤ 2°): low‑T 6.9–12.3%, high‑T 19.4–25.8%.
- Dislocation density indicator (KAM): low‑T 0.48–0.55°, high‑T 0.3–0.4°.
- HAGB fraction: low‑T 43.5–49.6%, high‑T 60.1–75.7%.
