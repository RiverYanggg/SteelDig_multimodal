Panel (d) presents engineering stress-strain curves revealing that ultimate tensile strength increases from 868.7 MPa (R650) to 1254.5 MPa (R950) at the expense of elongation, which drops from 19.0% to 3.5%. Panels (e) and (f) illustrate work-hardening rate curves versus true strain; low-temperature rolled specimens (R650/R750) exhibit a characteristic \"hump\" shape attributed to the Orowan mechanism of carbide-dislocation interaction, while high-temperature specimens (R850/R950) show monotonic decay without strain hardening capacity. These data collectively demonstrate that ultra-fine spheroidized carbides dispersed in ferrite/carbide composite lamellae at lower rolling temperatures enable superior impact toughness through enhanced crack blunting and deflection mechanisms, whereas high-temperature processing produces martensite/DIF microstructures with severe hardness mismatch leading to brittle fracture.", "confidence": "high"}
