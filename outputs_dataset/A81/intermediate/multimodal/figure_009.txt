The top row displays two-dimensional distribution maps of Fe, C, Mn (labeled as M), Al, and Si, where color contrast indicates elemental concentration (blue representing lower concentration and green/yellow higher concentration). The bottom row shows quantitative line-scan data extracted along the red dashed arrows indicated in the carbon maps, plotting atomic percentages of Si, Al, Mn, and C as functions of distance. The shaded regions in line scans correspond to specific phases: B/TM (bainite/tempered martensite), RA (retained austenite), and FM (fresh martensite). The data demonstrate that carbon is primarily enriched in the harder phases (B/TM, FM, and RA) while being depleted in ferrite. With increasing annealing temperature from 750°C to 850°C, the carbon distribution becomes progressively more uniform across the microstructure, whereas Mn, Al, and Si show relatively homogeneous distributions regardless of temperature. These results support the quantitative phase composition strategy by revealing the elemental partitioning behavior critical for understanding phase stability and mechanical properties.", "confidence": "high"}
