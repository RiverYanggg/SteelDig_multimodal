The images are organized in three rows: (a₁-a₅) for the 0Cu alloy, (b₁-b₅) for the 1Cu alloy, and (c₁-c₅) for the 3Cu alloy, with numerical subscripts 1 through 5 representing days 1 to 5 respectively. For the 0Cu alloy, isolated corrosion pits nucleate preferentially at carbide particles and gradually expand from small localized sites on day 1 to extensive corrosion zones exceeding 100μm by day 5. The 1Cu alloy exhibits chain-like connected corrosion pits aligned along grain boundaries, indicating intergranular corrosion propagation that intensifies progressively, with severe pit coalescence visible by day 5. In contrast, the 3Cu alloy displays a more uniform corrosion pattern characterized by a network of fine corrosion products decorating grain boundaries alongside dispersed pitting sites, suggesting enhanced microgalvanic activity between Cu-enriched grain boundaries and the austenitic matrix.
