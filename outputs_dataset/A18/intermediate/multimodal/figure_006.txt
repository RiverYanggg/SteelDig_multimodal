{"image_type": "microscopy_tem", "description": "Figure 4 presents the microstructural evolution of Fe(x)Mn11Al1C low-density steels with varying Mn content (20%, 25%, 30%, and 35%) after solution treatment at 1050°C for 2 h. Panels (a–d) show EBSD grain boundary maps where black lines denote high-angle boundaries (θ>15°), red lines indicate Σ3 twin boundaries, and green areas represent BCC ferrite phase. The images reveal that ferrite volume fraction decreases from 3.3% at 20% Mn to 0.65% at 25% Mn, disappearing completely at 30% and 35% Mn, while austenite grain size increases from 9.9 μm to 33 μm. Twin boundary fraction remains approximately 60% across samples. Panels (e–h) display dark-field TEM images (with corresponding SAED patterns in insets) showing κ-carbide precipitates imaged along [001]γ using κ-carbide reflections. The κ-carbide size decreases significantly with increasing Mn content: from approximately 18 nm at 20% Mn to 5 nm, 3 nm, and 2 nm at 25%, 30%, and 35% Mn, respectively. The SAED patterns confirm the coherent orientation relationship (001)κ||(001)γ. These results demonstrate that increasing Mn content promotes complete austenitization, grain coarsening, and simultaneous refinement of nano-scale κ-carbides.", "confidence": "high"}
