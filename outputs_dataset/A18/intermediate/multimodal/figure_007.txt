Panels (a–d) display EBSD orientation maps (inverse pole figure or boundary maps) revealing high-angle grain boundaries (black lines, θ>15°), annealing twin boundaries (red lines, Σ3), and BCC phase distribution (green regions). The 8–11% Al alloys exhibit single-phase austenite with average grain sizes of approximately 28.3 μm, 29.7 μm, and 23.7 μm, respectively, whereas the 12% Al alloy (d) shows significantly refined austenite grains (12.6 μm) containing 3.1% BCC ferrite phase. Panels (e–h) provide transmission electron microscopy evidence of κ-carbide precipitation. The 8% Al sample (e) shows a bright-field TEM image with an inset selected area electron diffraction (SAED) pattern along the [001]γ zone axis, confirming the absence of κ-carbides. Dark-field TEM images (f–h) taken using κ-carbide diffraction spots reveal uniformly distributed nanoscale precipitates (white contrast) in 10–12% Al steels, with average diameters increasing from approximately 1 nm (10% Al) to 1.5 nm (11% Al) and 15 nm (12% Al). These observations demonstrate that increasing Al content promotes κ-carbide formation and coarsening, while the presence of ferrite at 12% Al induces substantial grain refinement.", "confidence": "high"}
