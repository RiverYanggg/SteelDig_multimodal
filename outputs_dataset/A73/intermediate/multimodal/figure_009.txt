Subfigure (a) displays the sample sintered for 1 hour, while subfigure (b) shows the sample sintered for 3 hours. Both images reveal a fully austenitic matrix characterized by polygonal grains with visible annealing twins, alongside dark manganese oxide particles distributed both at grain boundaries and within grains. The scale bar indicates 200 μm. The micrographs demonstrate that the MIM process produces a fully austenitic structure with uniformly distributed manganese oxide particles, regardless of sintering duration within the examined range. These manganese oxide precipitates appear as black contrasting phases against the gray austenitic matrix. The images support the conclusion that MIM fabrication can achieve homogeneous microstructures in Fe–Mn–C–Al steels, which is crucial for enhancing wear-resistant properties through subsequent composite reinforcement with ultrahard particles such as SiC or alumina.", "confidence": "high"}
