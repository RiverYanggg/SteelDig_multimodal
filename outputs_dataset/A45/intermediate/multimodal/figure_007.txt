Subfigures A1 and A2 display the engineering and true stress-strain curves, respectively, showing that annealing significantly improves ductility compared to the as-hot-rolled condition, with the 60-minute sample exhibiting the highest ultimate tensile strength (~658 MPa) and elongation (~12%). Subfigures B1-B4 illustrate the corresponding work hardening rate (WHR) curves as a function of true strain, each revealing two distinct deformation stages: S1 (sharp decrease associated with δ-ferrite deformation) and S2 (linear decrease due to competing softening and hardening mechanisms, specifically the TRIP effect from austenite-to-martensite transformation). The mean WHR in stage S2 increases with annealing time from 1100-400 MPa (5 min) to 2000-1000 MPa (60 min), indicating enhanced austenite stability and TRIP effect, before decreasing to 1500-900 MPa at 180 minutes due to austenite dissolution.
