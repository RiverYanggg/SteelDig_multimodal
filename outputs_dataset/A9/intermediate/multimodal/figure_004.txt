{"image_type": "microscopy_sem", "description": "Figure 2 presents scanning electron microscopy (SEM) micrographs illustrating the microstructural evolution of near-rapidly solidified Fe-20Mn-9Al-1.2C-xCr low-density steel strips. Subfigures (a)-(c) display the as-cast microstructures of 0Cr, 3Cr, and 6Cr alloys, respectively, while subfigures (d)-(f) show the corresponding annealed conditions (600°C for 3 hours). The images reveal a dual-phase microstructure comprising γ-austenite matrix (darker gray regions) and irregularly shaped δ-ferrite particles (lighter contrast, indicated by arrows). The volume fraction of δ-ferrite visibly increases with Cr addition, evolving from approximately 3% in as-cast 0Cr to 12% in 3Cr and 18% in 6Cr, with further increases to 6%, 14%, and 24% respectively after annealing. Concurrently, Cr addition suppresses κ-carbide precipitation within the austenite matrix. All micrographs share a consistent scale bar of 20 μm. These observations demonstrate that Cr promotes ferrite formation and stabilizes the microstructure during both solidification and subsequent heat treatment.", "confidence": "high"}
