Panel (a) shows as-cast conditions, while panel (b) displays samples annealed at 600°C for 3 hours. The diffraction peaks are indexed as γ-austenite (fcc, red labels), δ-ferrite (bcc, blue labels), and ordered DO3 phase (green labels). In the as-cast state, all samples exhibit a dual-phase structure of γ and δ. Notably, the double-peak feature between 43.5°–45.0° corresponds to overlapping (110)δ and (220)DO3 reflections, indicating partial ordering in δ-ferrite. With increasing Cr content from 0Cr to 6Cr, the δ-ferrite volume fraction increases (evident from enhanced δ peaks), while κ-carbide precipitation weakens (confirmed by TEM in the text). After annealing, the double-peak transforms into a single DO3 superlattice peak, indicating complete ordering of δ-ferrite. Additionally, annealing promotes κ-carbide precipitation in 0Cr and 3Cr samples, evidenced by subtle superlattice reflections. These phase evolution trends demonstrate that Cr addition stabilizes δ-ferrite and suppresses κ-carbide formation, critically influencing the mechanical behavior of these lightweight steels.", "confidence": "high"}
