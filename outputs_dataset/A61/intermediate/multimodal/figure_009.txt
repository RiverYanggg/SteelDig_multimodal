The micrographs compare four sample conditions: solution-treated (ST), aged at 550°C for 6 h (550-6), aged at 600°C for 6 h (600-6), and aged at 650°C for 6 h (650-6), tested at stress amplitudes of Δσ/2 = 180 MPa (a-d) and 315 MPa (e-h). The transition from ductile to mixed-mode fracture correlates with extensive intergranular κ-carbide precipitation in the 650-6 condition, while the lower aging temperatures (550-6 and 600-6) maintain predominantly intragranular precipitation and superior toughness.
