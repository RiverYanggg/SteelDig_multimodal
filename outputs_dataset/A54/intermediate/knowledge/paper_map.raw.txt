At 600 °C, oxidation followed an initial parabolic stage then accelerated, accompanied by internal oxidation and nodule formation, whereas at 700–900 °C no internal oxidation occurred and two distinct parabolic rate regimes were observed. The results indicate that temperature strongly influences Al diffusion and the stability of carbide phases, governing the transition from internal oxidation to protective scale formation.",
