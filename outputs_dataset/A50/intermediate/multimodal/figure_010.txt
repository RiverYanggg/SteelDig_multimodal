{"image_type": "microscopy_tem", "description": "This figure presents a comprehensive multiscale analysis of deformation microstructures in Fe-35Mn-6Al-0.1C low-carbon low-density steel following impact testing at room temperature (RT, a–e) and liquid nitrogen temperature (LNT, f–j). Macroscopic fracture appearances (a, f) reveal extensive necking and numerous subordinate cracks at RT compared to limited necking and fewer cracks at LNT. EBSD phase maps (b, g) confirm the stability of austenite (γ) with approximately 3% ferrite (α) and absence of martensitic transformation at both temperatures, with red arrows in (g) indicating crack initiation at γ/α interfaces at LNT. Corresponding IPF maps (c, h) along the impact direction illustrate grain orientation evolution and deformation twinning, which is more pronounced at cryogenic temperatures. TEM micrographs (d, i) captured approximately 1000 μm from the fracture surface reveal dislocation substructures, microbands, and deformation twins with SAED patterns confirming the FCC austenite structure. SEM fractographs (e, j) display micrometer-scale dimples characteristic of ductile fracture at both temperatures. The figure demonstrates that while low temperature promotes deformation twinning, the γ/α interface serves as crack initiation sites at LNT, reducing impact energy from 331 J at RT to 254 J at LNT, yet maintaining ductile fracture characteristics.", "confidence": "high"}
