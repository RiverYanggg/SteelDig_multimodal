Subfigures (a) through (h) correspond to alloys containing 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30, 35, and 40 mass% Mn, respectively, all prepared by air induction melting and investment casting without subsequent heat treatment. The images reveal a macroscopic mixture of austenite (γ) phase (dark regions) and ferrite (α) phase (white regions), with all samples exhibiting characteristic dendritic morphologies. As Mn content increases from 5% to 40%, the volume fraction of the α phase progressively decreases from approximately 55% to nearly 0%, following the quantitative relationship α% = 0.0244(Mn%)² - 2.744(Mn%) + 68.65 as determined by image analysis. Notably, the alloy with 40 mass% Mn (h) essentially becomes a single γ-phase microstructure.
