Subfigure (a) shows the microstructure after 10% cold rolling reduction, revealing a Taylor lattice structure composed of multiple parallel slip bands. Green arrows indicate dislocation pile-ups at the intersections of slip bands, where dislocations are approximately parallel within individual bands, demonstrating the planar dislocation glide mechanism. Subfigure (b) displays the microstructure after 30% cold rolling reduction, showing a gradual increase in slip band density without obvious structural variation compared to (a). The average slip band spacing is measured as 231 nm at 30% reduction (caption reports 64 nm at 50% reduction). These images support the core conclusion that when true stress is below ~1.2 GPa, microstructural evolution is characterized by refinement of slip band spacing in the Taylor lattice, associated with planar dislocation glide resulting from \"glide plane softening\" effects. The weak interaction between non-coplanar slip bands allows dislocations to cross intersections, enabling slip bands to traverse entire grains throughout the deformation process.", "confidence": "high"}
