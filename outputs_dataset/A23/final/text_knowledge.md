## Material System
Fe–30Mn–11Al–1.2C (wt%) low-density austenitic steel, produced via vacuum induction melting, homogenized at 1150°C, hot-forged to 20 mm thick plates (finish forging temperature ~900°C), and solution-treated at 1100°C for 1 h followed by water quenching. The initial as-quenched microstructure consists of equiaxed austenite grains of ~70 μm average size containing annealing twins and nano-sized κ′-carbides formed by spinodal decomposition, uniformly distributed within the matrix (foil normal parallel to [001]γ). The estimated stacking fault energy (SFE) is approximately 108 mJ/m².

## Processing Route and Variables
- **Initial processing**: Vacuum induction melting → homogenization (1150°C) → hot forging to 20 mm plates (finish ~900°C) → solution treatment (1100°C, 1 h, water quench)
- **Cold rolling**: Thickness reductions of 10%, 20%, 30%, 40%, 50%, 70%, and 90%
- **Mechanical testing**: Uniaxial tensile tests at room temperature, initial strain rate 10⁻³ s⁻¹
- **Characterization orientation**: Longitudinal section (RD×ND plane)
- **Characterization methods**: Optical microscopy (OM), electron backscatter diffraction (EBSD), transmission electron microscopy (TEM) including bright-field (BF) / dark-field (DF) imaging and selected-area electron diffraction (SADP), X-ray diffraction (XRD) texture analysis via orientation distribution function (ODF)

## Microstructure and Phase Evolution

**Initial state (0% reduction, as-quenched)**:
- Equiaxed austenite grains, average size ~70 μm (including twins)
- Backscatter diffraction (EBSD) shows nearly random crystallographic texture
- High density of annealing twins (red lines in EBSD phase map)
- Nano-sized κ′-carbides distributed uniformly in matrix (TEM dark-field / SAED, [001]γ zone axis)

**Low-to-medium reductions (10%–50%)**:
- 10%: Taylor lattice structure with multiple parallel slip bands; dislocation pile-ups at slip band intersections; planar dislocation glide mechanism dominant
- 30%: Increased slip band density without structural transformation; average slip band spacing ~231 nm
- 50%: Slip band spacing refined to ~64 nm; onset of deformation twinning; microbands with average width ~166 nm (BF-TEM); nano-sized deformation twins, average thickness ~5.1 nm (DF-TEM using {−111} reflection, SADP [011] zone axis confirms twin–matrix relationship with indexed reflections (1−11)M, (−111)T, (111)T/M); high dislocation density within twins

**High reductions (70%–90%)**:
- 70%: Deformation twins coarsened to average thickness ~19.2 nm (DF-TEM); twin diffraction spots in SADP; shear bands intersecting deformation twins (BF-TEM); shear bands contain ultrafine grains (ring diffraction pattern in inset SAED)
- 90%: Dislocation tangles and cell structures with average cell size ~0.49 μm (BF-TEM); activation of dislocation cross-slip; extensive shear band formation eroding the matrix; pronounced grain elongation along RD

## Processing–Structure–Property Chain

**Cold rolling reduction → Microstructure → Mechanical response**:
- Solution-treated (0%): Yield strength ~570 MPa; ultimate tensile strength ~830 MPa; uniform elongation exceeding 75%
- At true stress below ~1.2 GPa (low-to-medium reductions, <50%): Microstructural evolution dominated by refinement of slip band spacing within Taylor lattice framework via planar dislocation glide; work hardening via slip band density increase
- 50% reduction: Transition signaled by onset of deformation twinning and microband formation; 166 nm microbands and 5.1 nm nano-twins emerge
- 70–90% reduction: Yield strength increases progressively, reaching ~2.1 GPa at 90% reduction; pronounced strength–ductility trade-off; true stress at necking points similar for reductions <50%, then sharply increases during further rolling; work hardening capability diminishes
- Strengthening mechanisms transition: Taylor lattice hardening (low strains) → combined twinning + shear banding → Hall–Petch grain refinement via dislocation cells (0.49 μm, 90%)

## Mechanistic Interpretation

**Planar dislocation glide (Stage I, <50% reduction)**:
- "Glide plane softening" effect promotes planar slip on active slip planes
- Weak interaction between non-coplanar slip bands; dislocations can cross slip band intersections, allowing slip bands to traverse entire grains
- Taylor lattice structure forms as low-energy dislocation structure (LEDS)

**Deformation twinning despite high SFE (~108 mJ/m²)**:
- Deformation twinning occurs at 50% reduction and above, although SFE is higher than the typical threshold (~40 mJ/m²) for twinning-induced plasticity (TWIP) in high-Mn steels
- Attributed to short-range order (SRO) effects associated with Al and C in solid solution, plus local stress concentrations from planar slip band intersections
- Twins form fine T–M (twin–matrix) lamella structures contributing to strain hardening

**Texture transition: Copper-type → Brass-type**:
- Occurring beyond 50% reduction, linked to activation of deformation twinning and micro-shear bands
- Copper-type texture characterized by Copper {112}〈111〉 and S {123}〈634〉 components dominant at low-to-medium reductions
- Brass-type texture characterized by Brass {110}〈112〉, Goss {110}〈100〉, and CuT {552}〈115〉 components strengthening at high reductions
- α-fiber: Brass increases with reduction and scatters toward Goss; maximum intensity shifts from φ₁=45° to ~20° at >50% reduction
- τ-fiber: Cu peaks at 50% then decreases with Φ shift to higher values; weak CuT emerges at 70% with subsequent Φ decrease
- γ-fiber remains weak across all reductions, slightly strengthened at 90% at E position ({111}〈110〉)
- β-fiber: Cu and S components initially increase then decrease (S reduction delayed until 70%); Goss monotonously increases

**Shear banding and dislocation cell formation (Stage II, >50% reduction)**:
- Shear bands develop at 70% reduction, intersecting deformation twins and containing ultrafine grains
- At 90%, dislocation cross-slip activates, forming well-defined dislocation cells (average size 0.49 μm) within shear bands and matrix

## Key Quantitative Findings

- **Stacking fault energy**: ~108 mJ/m²
- **Initial grain size**: ~70 μm (as-quenched), equiaxed, with annealing twins
- **Slip band spacing**: ~231 nm at 30% → ~64 nm at 50% reduction
- **Microband width**: ~166 nm (average, at 50% reduction)
- **Nano-twin thickness**: ~5.1 nm at 50% → ~19.2 nm at 70% reduction
- **Dislocation cell size**: ~0.49 μm (average, at 90% reduction)
- **Yield strength evolution**: ~570 MPa (0%) → ~2.1 GPa (90% reduction)
- **Ultimate tensile strength**: ~830 MPa (solution-treated)
- **Uniform elongation**: >75% (solution-treated)
- **Texture transition threshold**: ~50% cold rolling reduction (copper-type → brass-type)
