Subfigures (a–c) at lower magnification (scale bar 20 μm) display micron-sized polygonal (Nb,Mo,W)C particles aligned along the rolling direction, while (d–f) at higher magnification (scale bar 300 nm) show submicron-sized particles homogeneously distributed within the austenite matrix. The rows correspond to samples HR45 (45% reduction), HR68 (68% reduction), and HR82 (82% reduction), respectively. The bright white contrast indicates the (Nb,Mo,W)C carbides, which are incoherent with the FCC austenite matrix and range from approximately 260 nm (submicron) to 1–25 μm (micron-sized) with a volume fraction of ~0.2%. These hard particles serve multiple roles: they act as preferential nucleation sites for particle-stimulated nucleation (PSN) during dynamic recrystallization, contributing to grain refinement with increasing rolling reduction; they provide precipitation strengthening via Orowan bypass mechanisms; however, they may also induce strain/stress concentration at interfaces, potentially serving as microcrack initiation sites during deformation.", "confidence": "high"}
